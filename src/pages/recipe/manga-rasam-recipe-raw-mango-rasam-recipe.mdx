### Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon Lemon juice.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 cup Water.
- 1 Mango (Raw).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 Green Chillies - slit.
- 1/2 teaspoon Black pepper powder - freshly ground.
- 1/4 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal) - cooked.
- 1 Tomato - diced.
- 5 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- Asafoetida (hing) - a pinch.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1 tablespoon Jaggery.
- 1 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Mango Rasam Recipe, firstly wash the mango, cut the stem and place the entire mango in the pressure cooker along with 1/2 cup of water.
1. Close the cooker with lid.
1. Pressure cook for 5 to 6 whistles and allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. When the pressure releases and the mango has cooled, squeeze the pulp out and discard the stone and peels.
1.  Add the mango pulp to a mixer grinder, along with half a cup of water and blend to make smooth mango puree.
1. Keep aside.
1. Cook the toor dal in 1 cup of water for about 5 to 6 whistles and allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Once the pressure releases, mash the toor dal until smooth and keep aside.
1. Next, add the ghee in a saucepan.
1. Once the ghee is hot, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds and allow it to crackle.
1. Once the seeds have crackled add the asafoetida, curry leaves, green chillies, ginger and saute for a few seconds.
1. Add the mango pulp, tomato, lemon juice, black pepper powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, jaggery, cooked dal, salt to taste and mix everything well.
1. Add about 2 cups of water to adjust the consistency of the rasam.
1. Bring the Mango Rasam to a brisk boil for about 10 minutes till you see froth around the edges and turn off the heat.
1. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves, give it a mix and transfer Mango Rasam into a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. Serve Mango Rasam Recipe along with Steamed Rice, Beetroot Poriyal and Elai Vadam for a weekday meal.
