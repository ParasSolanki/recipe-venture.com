### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder - or green chilli paste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil - hot.
- 1/2 cup Whole Wheat Flour.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 cup Bajra Flour ( Pearl Millet).
- 2 teaspoon Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 2 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 1 teaspoon Garlic - paste.
- 1 cup Methi Leaves (Fenugreek Leaves) - cleaned.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Methi Bajra Poori, firstly mix all the ingredients except the oil for deep frying, in a mixing bowl or in the bowl of a food processor.
1. Gradually add water and knead it into a firm yet pliable dough.
1. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
1. Make small balls from the dough.
1. Roll them out into small round discs.
1. Keep them covered till they are ready to be fried.
1. Heat oil in a Kadhai/Wok.
1. Gently slide in the rolled poori or 2-3 pooris (depending on the size of your wok).
1. Deep fry on medium high heat until they puff and turn golden brown on both sides.
1. Take out the pooris using a slotted spoon and place on absorbent paper.
1. Serve hot.
1. Serve Methi Bajra Poori on its own as a snack or it can be teamed up with Aloo Dum or any other curry for an indulgent meal.
