### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 2 Green Chilli - chopped.
- 2 Stone flower.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 Bay leaves (tej patta).
- 4 cloves Garlic - chopped.
- 1 inch Ginger - chopped.
- 2 Barnyard Millet - soaked.
- 5 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves.
- 2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 2 Tomatoes - chopped.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 2 Onion - sliced.
- 5 sprig Mint Leaves (Pudina).
- 1 Star anise.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 300 grams Button mushrooms - quartered.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Barnyard Millet Mushroom Biryani, we will first grind the ingredients mentioned in the list to a fine paste and keep it aside.
1. Heat a kadai with oil, add all the whole spice first and leave it for 10 seconds for the aroma to be released.
1. Add the curry leaves and allow it splutter, add chopped onions, ginger, garlic and saute them until it softens.
1. Once the onions softens, add chopped tomatoes, little bit of salt so that the tomatoes will get cooked faster.
1. When the tomatoes become mushy, add the ground green paste and add all the spice powders mentioned in the list.
1. Once the masala start to bubble, you can add chopped mushroom and give it a toss.
1. Add about Â¼ cup of water and allow the mushroom to cook with the masalas.
1. Later add the barnyard millet and 2 cups of water and check for salt.
1. Stir well and cover it with the lid and cook in low heat for about 15 minutes.
1. After 15 minutes, you can open the lid and mix the millet rice with the masala well and serve.
1. Serve the Barnyard Millet Mushroom Biryani Recipe along with a spinach raitha, cucumber salad to go along with your meal.
