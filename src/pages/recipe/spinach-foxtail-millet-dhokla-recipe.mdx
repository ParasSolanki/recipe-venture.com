### Ingredients

- 1 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 1 teaspoon Enos fruit salt - or 1/2 teaspoon baking powder.
- 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 1 Green Chilli.
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil - for seasoning.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 inch Ginger.
- 1 tablespoon Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 1 cup Gram flour (besan).
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves - finely chopped.
- 1 cup Foxtail Millet - ground.
- 2 cups Spinach.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Spinach Foxtail Millet Dhokla Recipe, Prepare a dhokla steamer, fill with water and grease the dhokla plates.
1. Keep aside.
1. Grind the spinach, cumin seeds, green chilli and ginger in a mixer and add some water to make it into a smooth puree.
1. Combine the foxtail millet flour, besan, spinach puree, yogurt and a little salt in a large mixing bowl.
1. Add a little water at a time to make a thick batter (approximately 1- 1/2 cups).
1. Allow the mixture to rest for 5 minutes.
1. Stir in the Enos fruit salt and pour the dhokla batter into the greased dhokla platesPreheat a steamer with water and place the dhokla batter for steaming.
1. Steam on high heat for about 10 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
1. Once steamed, remove the Spinach Foxtail Millet Dhokla from the steamer and keep aside for 5 minutes.
1. Heat oil in a tadka pan; add in the mustard seeds, sesame seeds and curry leaves and allow it to crackle.
1. Turn off heat and pour the seasoning over the dhoklas and cut them into squares or diamonds.
1. Serve the Ragi Rava Dhokla recipe along with Green Chutney and also Idli Milagai Podi.
1. I even send this as a school lunch box as well and the kids love it.
