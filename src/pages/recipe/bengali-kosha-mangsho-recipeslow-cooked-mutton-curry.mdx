### Ingredients

- 2 teaspoon Lemon juice.
- 2 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 3 Tomato - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 5 cloves Garlic - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Ginger - grated.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 4 Onions - chopped.
- 1 Bay leaves (tej patta) - torn.
- 3 Potatoes (Aloo) - halved (optional).
- 750 grams Mutton.
- 3 Cloves (Laung).
- 8 cloves Garlic.
- 2 teaspoons Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 inch Ginger.
- 1 Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 4 tablespoon Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).

### Instructions

1. We begin making Bengali Kosha Mangsho Recipe-Slow Cooked Mutton Curry Recipe by marinating the mutton with ground ginger and garlic paste.
1. Then add the yogurt and the rest of the spice powders and mix it thoroughly till it is evenly coated.
1. Rest it for 2 hours.
1. Then heat  the pan with mustard oil, saute onion and tomato till it is well done.
1. Add all the spice powders once done keep it aside to cool and blend it into a smooth paste using a blender.
1. Heat the pressure cooker, add oil, temper it with black pepper, cinnamon stick,bay leaves cardamom and sugar.
1. Add in the onion and tomato paste with all the masala powders, fry about 5 minutes.
1. Now add the marinated mutton and the potatoes coat it with the gravy.
1. Cook at a low heat, add 1 cup of water and cook the mutton covered for 5 whistles.
1. Once the pressure has been released naturally garnish with some chopped coriander leaves.
1. Serve your spicy flavorful  Bengali Kosha Mangsho-Slow Cooked Mutton Curry with Lachha Paratha.
