### Ingredients

- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Enos fruit salt.
- 5 grams Tamarind.
- Salt - to taste.
- 3 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- 6 Badi hari mirch (Large Green Chilli).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 Onion - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1 cup Gram flour (besan).
- 2 tablespoon Roasted Gram Dal (Pottukadalai).
- 1/3 cup Rice flour.
- 1 teaspoon Chaat Masala Powder.
- 1 pinch Asafoetida (hing).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Andhra Style Mirapakaya bajji Recipe, wash the chillies and cut them in the middle.
1. Deseed them and keep it aside.
1. Be sure to wash your hands after deseeding.
1. In a mixer jar combine the roasted chana dal, red chilli powder, cumin seeds, ajwain seeds, chaat masala, tamarind, hing and grind it a mixer by adding 2 tablespoons of water to a smooth mixture.
1. Stuff this mixture into each of the hollow chilies.
1. Move on to make the batter by mixing all the ingredients- besan, rice flour, red chilli powder, enos salt, salt in a bowl with 1/2 cup of water to a smooth thick batter.
1. Heat a kadai with oil to deep fry the chillies on medium-low flame.
1. Check if the oil is hot by dropping a little amount of batter into the oil.
1. If the batter rises up quickly then it is hot enough.
1. Dip the green chilies into the batter and coat it evenly, carefully drop the chilies into the hot oil and fry.
1. Turn the chilies after 30 seconds so that it has an even brown color all over.
1. Take out the chilies using a slotted spoon and place it on a kitchen paper towel to drain the oil.
1. Do the same for the rest of the chilies.
1. Mix the onion and coriander leaves together and add it on top as a garnish.
1. Serve the Andhra Style Mirapakaya bajji Recipe along with hot cup of tea for your evening snack.
