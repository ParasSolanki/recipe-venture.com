### Ingredients

- 8 sprig Green Amaranth Leaves - roughly chopped.
- 1/2 tablespoons Jaggery.
- 1/2 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 2 Tomatoes - roughly chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 Tamarind - small lemon sized.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal) - boiled.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 Onion - sliced.
- 1 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 5 cloves Garlic - finely sliced.
- 2 Byadagi Dried Chillies.
- 2 teaspoons White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 2 teaspoons Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Harive Soppina Saaru Recipe, wash and soak the toor dal for 30 minutes.
1. Boil the toor dal, along with 2 cloves of garlic, turmeric, cumin seeds for 3 whistles using a pressure cooker.
1. Once the dal is cooked, whisk it with a ladle to smooth texture.
1. Add the tomatoes, chopped amaranth leaves and cook for another whistle in the pressure cooker.
1. Now, to make the masala, roast all the ingredients mentioned in the table except grated coconut and tamarind till aromatic using little oil and keep aside.
1. Make a fine paste of the roasted ingredients along with coconut and tamarind using a mixer grinder with little water.
1. Add this masala to the boiled dal and amaranth mix, enough water to your desired consistency, jaggery, season with salt and simmer for 10 minutes and switch off the flame.
1. Now, heat oil in a small tadka pan, add the mustard, cumin seeds and let it splutter.
1. Add the red chilli, curry leaves and let it crackle.
1. Add the sliced garlic and saute till they are golden and crisp.
1. Add in the sliced onion and saute till they are brown.
1. Now, add this tadka over the prepared dal and amaranth curry and mix well.
1. Enjoy the Harive Soppina Saaru Recipe (Karnataka Style Amaranth Leaves Cooked with Lentils) along with Steamed Rice and Cabbage Palya for a weeknight dinner.
