### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1 tablespoon Sambar Powder.
- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- 1 cup Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - cubed.
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 2 cups Idli Dosa Batter.
- 1 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal) - washed.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 cup Red Bell pepper (Capsicum) - cubed.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 cup Pearl onions (Sambar Onions).
- 6 Curry leaves.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 cup Tamarind Water.
- 1/2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil - to saute veggies.
- 2 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 cup Yellow Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - cubed.
- 1/2 cup Bottle gourd (lauki) - cooked and finely chopped.
- Salt - to taste if needed.
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 3 Kashmiri dry red chillies - broken.
- 1 Drumstick - cooked and cut into medium pieces.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Idli Tikka Recipe, take the idli dosa batter, and keep ready.
1. Keep the idli steamer to preheat, with water inside.
1. Make sure that the water level does not touch or cross the idli plates when arranges inside.
1. Grease all the cavities of idli steaming pan and pour in the batter into each cavity.
1. Once done, steam the idlis for 10-15 minutes of full heat or till done.
1. Here I have used mini idli pan too do these idlis.
1. In a shallow frying pan, heat a teaspoon of oil.
1. Once hot, add mustard seeds, urad dal and curry leaves and allow it to crackle and the dal to turn golden brown and crisp.
1.  Add idlis in the pan and toss to coat.
1. Add salt only if needed.
1. Take care that the idlis don't break while tossing.
1. Saute lightly and turn off heat.
1. To Saute Veggies:In a shallow frying pan, heat a bit of oil and add all the vegetables and saute for 4-5 minutes on low-medium heat or till slightly tender but cooked al dente.
1. Keep aside.
1. On a wooden skewer, thread a piece of red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper then a green bell pepper, then one mini idli, and again do same ordering for second idli, and arrange them on a serving bowl.
1. To make SambarTo prepare sambar, in a pressure cooker, cook arhar dal with enough water, to about 3 whistles or till mushy.
1. Allow the pressure to release by itself.
1. Once the pressure has released, mash the dal and keep aside.
1. Transfer the dal to another bowl and keep aside.
1. Into the pressure cooker, add the onions, vegetables, turmeric powder, sambar powder, salt and tamarind water.
1. Pressure cook for 2 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. Add the cooked dal to this mixture and give it a brisk boil.
1. Check the salt and spices and adjust according to taste.
1. In a tadka pan, heat a teaspoon of oil.
1. Once hot, add the ingredients to temper the sambar and wait until they splutter well and switch off heat.
1. Pour this on the sambar and give it a stir.
1. Pour sambar to the bowl and enjoy the idlis and bell peppers after dunking them in! Serve Idli Tikka Recipe alongside a bowl of South Indian Coconut Chutney and Peanut Chutney Powder Recipe for dinner or for a Sunday brunch to spice up your weekend.
