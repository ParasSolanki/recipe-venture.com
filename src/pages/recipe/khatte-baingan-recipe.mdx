### Ingredients

- 1 Tamarind - small lemon sized ball.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 2 Tomatoes - deseeded and pureed.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 250 grams Small Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - (crown removed and cut into 4 pieces each).
- 1/2 tablespoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 4 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 3 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- Sunflower Oil - for frying.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Khatte Baingan recipe, wash and pat dry the cut Brinjals with a towel.
1. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the brinjals till they turn soft and slightly change colour.
1. Once done, transfer the fried brinjals to a plate and keep ready.
1. Wash and extract the pulp of tamarind and keep this tamarind water ready as well.
1. Now heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and mustard seeds and saute for a minute.
1. Next add black peppercorns and sesame seeds and saute for a few seconds.
1. Then add ginger garlic paste and saut√© till the raw smell goes.
1. Add tomato puree, red chilli powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder and salt and mix well.
1. Cook till oil starts separating.
1. Then add coriander leaves and tamarind water.
1. Bring the gravy to a boil and cook for a couple of minutes.
1. Finally add the fried Brinjals, mix well and cook for a minute.
1. Then reduce heat, close a lid and cook for about 5 minutes till the brinjals are cooked and have absorbed the masalas.
1. When the brinjals have cooked and the gravy has become thick, switch off the gas.
1. Transfer the Khatte Baingan to a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. Serve Khatte Baingan along with Panchmel Dal, Kachumber Salad and Phulkas for a weekday meal.
