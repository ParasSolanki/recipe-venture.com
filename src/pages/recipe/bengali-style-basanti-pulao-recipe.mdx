### Ingredients

- 20 Raisins.
- 1 tablespoon Sugar.
- 2 teaspoons Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 20 Cashew nuts.
- 2 Bay leaves (tej patta).
- 2 cups Govind Bhog Rice - cleaned.
- 2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 2 tablespoons Ghee.
- 2 teaspoons Ginger - grated.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Bengali Style Basanti Pulao Recipe, smear the gobindobhog rice with turmeric and keep aside for 20 minutes for the rice to absorb the gorgeous yellow.
1. Heat a tablespoon each of ghee and vegetable oil in deep fry pan.
1. On low heat, fry the cashewnuts and raisins till they turn golden brown, keep aside.
1. In the same oil, on low medium heat, add the bay leaves, crushed cinnamon, green cardamom, cloves, saute till the spices start to release their aroma.
1. Add the ginger paste and saute for a minute or two; just about till the raw smell of ginger vanishes.
1. Now add the rice and give a gentle stir, taking care not to break the grains.
1. Saute gently for 3-5 minutes over a medium flame.
1. Add 4 cups of warm water (golden rule - rice and water should always be in a 1:2 ratio), add sugar and salt to taste.
1. Throw in the fried cashew nuts and raisins.
1. Cover and cook till almost all the water has been absorbed, by time the rice should be almost done.
1. Switch off the flame, add the remaining ghee, cover and allow to rest for a further 10-12 minutes.
1. The Basanti Pulao is ready.
1. Serve Bengali Style Basanti Pulao Recipe along with Lauki Raita Recipe and Kumro Patai Ilish Paturi Recipe.
1. To end the meal on a sweet note serve it with Rasgulla Recipe.
