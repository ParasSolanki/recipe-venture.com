### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Colocasia root (Arbi) - cut into cubes.
- 1 teaspoon Tamarind Paste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Sunflower Oil - as required.
- Salt - as per your taste.
- 1 Tomato - finely choppes.
- 1 Carrot (Gajjar) - cut into cubes.
- 2 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1/4 cup Kaddu (Parangikai/ Pumpkin) - cut into cubes.
- 1 teaspoon Panch Phoran Masala.
- 1/4 cup Mooli/ Mullangi (Radish) - cut into cubes.
- 1/4 cup Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - cut into cubes.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 teaspoon Gram flour (besan).
- 2 Bhindi (Lady Finger/Okra) - cut into small pieces.
- 3 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1/4 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 Drumstick - cut into 2 inch pieces.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Ambila Recipe, in a bowl, mix curd/dahi and besan.
1. Add 4 teaspoons of water and mix well to make sure there are no lumps in it.
1. Next, dissolve the tamarind pulp in 3-4 teaspoons of warm water and keep aside.
1. Alternatively, you can also soak 20 grams or lemon sized tamarind in hot water and squeeze out the tamarind pulp.
1. Place a pan on medium heat and add 1 teaspoon oil.
1. Add the tomatoes and saute until the tomatoes are soft.
1. Remove the tomatoes and keep aside.
1. To the same pan add all the other vegetables, except drumstick and saute for a couple of minutes.
1. Turn off the flame and keep aside.
1. .
1. In a large vessel, add 5 cups of water and boil all the sauteed vegetables along with drum stick and tomatoes with turmeric powder and salt.
1. When the vegetables are half cooked (takes about 10 minutes), add the besan and yogurt mixture.
1. Mix well.
1. Next add the tamarind water and give it all a good mix.
1. Let the vegetables cook properly in a medium flame.
1. When the vegetables are cooked completely, switch off the flame.
1. In a tadka pan, heat up 2 tablespoons of oil.
1. Once the oil is hot, reduce the heat and add panch phoran masala.
1. When the masala starts to turn aromatic, add chopped garlic, whole dried red chillies and curry leaves.
1. Saut√© until the garlic turns to golden brown colour .
1. New add red chilli powder to the tadka pan, mix well and immediately pour this tempering to the cooked vegetables.
1. Mix the tadka well with the cooked vegetables and transfer into a serving bowl.
1. Ambila is ready to be served.
1. Serve Ambila along with Steamed Rice and a dollop of Ghee for a perfect weekday lunch.
