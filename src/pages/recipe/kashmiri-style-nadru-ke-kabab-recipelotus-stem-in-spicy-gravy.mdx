### Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger - minced/grated.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 Onion - small.
- 1 Potato (Aloo) - boiled and peeled.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 5 Tomatoes - medium sized.
- 1/2 Lemon juice.
- 1 Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - small.
- 2 tablespoon Homemade tomato puree.
- 2 inch Ginger - grated/ 2 teaspoons ginger paste.
- 1 cup Whole Wheat Bread crumbs - for coating.
- 1 teaspoon Garlic - paste.
- 2 tablespoon Roasted Gram Flour (Sattu Ka Atta).
- Red Chilli powder - for tempering.
- Water - for consistency.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 tablespoons Ghee - or clarified butter.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 3 tablespoons Tomato Ketchup.
- 2 pinch Ajwain (Carom seeds) - crushed between palms.
- 1/2 cup Cashew nuts.
- 1 Lotus Stem - washed.
- 7-8 Garlic.
- 2 Green Chillies - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Milk - optional.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).

### Instructions

1. To begin making your Nadru Ke Kabab, in a pressure cooker, add tomatoes, Ginger, garlic, onion, capsicum, cashew and 1/4th cup water (to avoid burning).
1. Pressure cook this till one whistle on high flame and the simmer and cook further till two more whistles.
1. Cool the contents and grind into a fine paste in a mixer grinder.
1. Sieve it and keep aside.
1. In a bowl, mix boiled potatoes and boiled lotus stem along with the rest of the ingredients for making kabab.
1. Mix well.
1. Now take a tablespoon full of this mix and roll between your hands to make a kabab.
1. Apply a little oil if sticky, but do not add any more flour.
1. Lotus stems and potatoes have a sticky texture so it’s fine.
1. Coat with bread crumbs.
1. Make all the kebabs like this and keep them in the refrigerator for 5 minutes.
1. Heat Oil in a Wok and start frying after refrigerating.
1. Fry on a medium-high flame to avoid oiliness.
1. Keep these aside till you make the gravy.
1. Heat ghee/ clarified butter in a pan.
1. Add the tomato puree, salt and garam masala.
1. Sauté this on simmer till it leaves ghee on the sides of the pan.
1. Add the Makhani mixture which we had pressure cooked earlier.
1. Add tomato ketchup and cook until done.
1. For Tempering, heat a little Ghee in a tadka pan.
1. One it's warm, add degi mirch, saute for 2-5 seconds and take the pan off heat.
1. Instruction for ServingIn a serving bowl, place the kebabs.
1. Now, pour over the prepared gravy.
1. Garnish with the red chilli tempering and coriander leaves.
1. Serve Nadru Ke Kabab along with Pudina Tawa Paratha for a weekday Lunch or dinner.
