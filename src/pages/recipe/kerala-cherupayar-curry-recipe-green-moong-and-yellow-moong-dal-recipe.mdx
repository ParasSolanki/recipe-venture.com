### Ingredients

- 2 Green Chilli.
- 1 cup Green Moong Dal (Whole).
- 10 Pearl onions (Sambar Onions).
- 2 Dry Red Chilli.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- Coconut Oil - for cooking.
- 1/2 cup Fresh coconut.
- 1/2 cup Yellow Moong Dal (Split).
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Kerala Cherupayar Curry Recipe, soak the green mung bean in water for at least 2 hours.
1. Pressure cook the green mung bean with water for about 4 whistle till it is just cooked.
1. Once the pressure has released naturally, take them out and pressure cook the yellow moong dal now by adding some water, turmeric powder and pressure cook for 4 whistle.
1. Wait for the pressure to release naturally and mash the dal to a smooth paste.
1. Heat a kadai with oil, add mustard seeds and allow it splutter for few seconds and add curry leaves and be careful as it will splutter quickly.
1. Add the whole shallots and saute until they soften.
1. Add mashed yellow moong dal along with cooked green moong dal and mix well.
1. Cook in simmer.
1. Meanwhile grind the coconut, green chilli, cumin seeds in a mixer and grind it into a smooth paste by adding little water.
1. Add this ground paste into the boiling dal, adjust your salt levels and boil for another 10 minutes and serve hot.
1. Serve the Kerala Cherupayar Curry Recipe along with puttu or steamed rice.
1. It can also be paired with phulka for a lunch box.
