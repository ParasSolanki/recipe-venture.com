### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta) - torn into half.
- 1 cup Homemade tomato puree.
- Salt - to taste.
- Sunflower Oil - for cooking.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 inch Ginger.
- 1 Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - cut into wedges.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - a small bunch finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Raisins.
- 1 tablespoon Fresh cream.
- 3 cloves Garlic.
- 1 Onion - roughly chopped.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Baingan Musallam Recipe, first prep all the ingredients required.
1. Make the tomato puree and keep aside.
1. Make the ginger, onion, garlic paste and keep aside.
1. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a Kadai/Heavy Bottomed Pan.
1. Add the eggplant wedges, sprinkle some salt and cook the baingan in the Pan until done.
1. Cover the pan, so that the steam that gets created will help cook the baingan faster.
1. Once the baingan is cooked, remove from the pan and keep aside.
1. In the same Pan, add the onion garlic paste and saute on medium heat until the raw smell goes away.
1. Add all the spices, the tomato puree and cook the tomato puree along with the spices for a couple of minutes until it comes to a brisk boil.
1. Stir in the raisins, the roasted baingan, the cream and simmer for about 5 minutes until the masalas get well incorporated into the baingan.
1. Once done, check the salt and spices and adjust the taste accordingly.
1. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve the Baingan Musallam Recipe along with Vegetable Yakhni Pulao Recipe and Burani Raita for a delicious dinner for parties.
