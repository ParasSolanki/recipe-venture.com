### Ingredients

- 4 Garlic.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 tablespoon Fresh cream.
- 1/2 teaspoon Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves).
- 1 Onions - roughly chopped.
- 1/4 cup Cashew nuts.
- 1 Green Chillies.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger - grated.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 300 grams Bhindi (Lady Finger/Okra) - cut in 1 inch length.
- 1/2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin (Jeera) seeds.
- 1 Tomato - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Shahi Bhindi Recipe in Cashew Nut Gravy - Okras in Cashew Nut Curry, first prepare the bhindi and get all the ingredients ready and keep aside.
1. Heat a teaspoon of oil in a heavy bottomed pan or a skillet.
1. Add the jeera and allow it to crackle.
1. Add cardamom, cinnamon stick, clove and stir for few seconds.
1. Add onions, garlic and ginger and saute until the onions are pink and soft.
1. Add cashew nuts, green chili and saute for a minute along with the onions.
1. Add tomatoes and saute well until all the raw smell goes off, tomatoes are soft and spices releases roasted aroma.
1. Once all the ingredients are softened and cooked, turn off the heat and allow it to cool.
1. Once cooled, grind this shahi masala mixture into a smooth paste.
1. Heat ghee in the same pan as above and add the cut bhindi.
1. Sprinkle some salt and stir saute them for about 5 minutes on medium heat until cooked through.
1. Do not allow them to burn but just roast them well.
1. Transfer the cooked bhindi into another bowl.
1. In the same pan,  heat 1 tablespoon of oil.
1. Add the ground shahi masala paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder and saute until the masala is cooked well.
1. Add the fried bhindi and stir to combine the bhindi well into the shahi masala.
1. Add  warm water to adjust the consistency of the gravy .
1. Bring the Shahi Bhindi to a brisk boil and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
1. Lastly, add 2 tablespoon of cream and kasuri methi.
1. Stir and cook for another minute and turn off the heat.
1. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve the Shahi Bhindi in Cashew Nut Gravy along with Parathas or Butter Naan, Jeera Rice and Sindhi Kadhi for a perfect weekend party with friends.
