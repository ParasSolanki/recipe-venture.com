### Ingredients

- 1 Onions - chopped.
- 4 cloves Garlic - crushed.
- 2 Dry Red Chilli.
- Sunflower Oil - for cooking.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 cup Pearl onions (Sambar Onions).
- 1 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns - crushed.
- 1 Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 1/2 cup Black Eyed Beans (Lobia) - soaked in water for 2 hours.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 1 Drumstick - cut into 2 inch stripes.

### Instructions

1. We begin making the Tamil Nadu Style Karamani Murungakkai Kuzhambu Recipe by pressure cooking the karamani (Black eyed beans) and drumstick in the pressure cooker with 1/4 cup water and salt for about 2 whistle.
1. Release the pressure naturally and keep it aside.
1. Heat a skillet with oil and fry the chopped onions, fennel seeds and dry red chilli till it is golden brown and cool it down.
1. Grind it into a smooth paste.
1. Heat a kadai, with oil and fry the whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, whole peppercorns) for few seconds till the aroma comes out.
1. Then add in the pearl onions and garlic, saut√© till it is translucent.
1. Add in the ground paste along with all the spice powders (coriander powder & red chilli powder).
1. Check for salt and you can add 1 cup water and stir well.
1. Add in the cooked beans and drumstick.
1. Stir well and simmer the Karamani Murungakkai Kuzhambu for at least 15 minutes and serve.
1. Serve the Tamil Nadu Style Karamani Murungakkai Kuzhambu Recipe can be served along with Dry Fry Keema Balls (Spicy Chicken Keema Balls) Recipe ,Thakkali Rasam Recipe (South Indian Tomato Rasam) and rice to make it a meal.
