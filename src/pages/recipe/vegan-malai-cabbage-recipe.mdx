### Ingredients

- 2 teaspoons Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves).
- 1 Green Chilli.
- 2 teaspoons Sunflower Oil.
- 2 cloves Garlic.
- 1/4 cup Cashew nuts - ground to a fine paste.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Black pepper powder.
- 1 inch Ginger.
- 1/4 cup Coconut milk.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 Green Chilli - sliced for garnish.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- Cardamom Powder (Elaichi) - a pinch.
- 1 Onion - roughly chopped.
- 2 cups Cauliflower (gobi) - cut into florets.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Vegan Malai Gobhi Recipe, clean and cut the cauliflower into small florets.
1. Soak it in warm water with little salt to de-worm and keep aside.
1. Make a fine paste of cashew with little water using a mixer grinder and keep aside.
1. Heat oil in a heavy bottomed pan, add cloves, bay leaf and let the oil become fragrant.
1. Make a fine paste of onion, ginger, garlic and green chilli using a blender and add it to the pan.
1. Let it cook until the raw smell goes away.
1. Drain the cauliflower florets from the warm water and add it to the pan.
1. Saute for a minute.
1. Add the garam masala, cardamom and pepper powder, 1 cup water and let the cauliflower florets cook to 3/4 the with the lid closed.
1. This will take about 8 to 10 minutes.
1. Add the cashew paste, season with salt and simmer for 2 minutes.
1. Add the coconut milk and adjust the consistency by adding little water and let it simmer for 5 minutes and switch off the heat.
1. Slightly crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the malai gobhi along with the green chillies as a garnish.
1. Serve the Vegan Malai Gobhi Recipe, along with Phulkas and Tomato Onion Cucumber Raita for the weekday lunch or dinner.
