### Ingredients

- 1 inch Ginger - chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Lukewarm Water - for kneading.
- 2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 cup Green peas (Matar) - ground into a paste.
- 1 Green Chilli - chopped.
- Sunflower Oil - for kneading.
- 1 cup Whole Wheat Flour.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 1 cup Paneer (Homemade Cottage Cheese) - crumbled.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Paneer and Green Peas Paratha prep all the  ingredients ready and keep them handy.
1. To make the dough for the paratha.
1. In a large mixing bowl, add the flour and salt.
1. Add water little at a time and knead to make a soft and smooth dough.
1. Once all the flour has come together, drizzle a teaspoon of oil on the top of the dough and knead for another couple of minutes.
1. Cover the dough and we will proceed to make the stuffing.
1. Heat a skillet on a medium heat with some oil, add cumin seeds and allow it splutter for few seconds.
1. Add in ginger, saute them well.
1. Then add in the peas.
1. Saute them till it is half cooked.
1. Add in the crumbled paneer and add all the spice powders, cook them well on the medium heat till the raw smell of the spice goes away.
1. Keep it aside to cool.
1. Divide the dough portions into medium lemon size balls.
1. Place some flour aside for dusting.
1. Pat the portion of the dough and dust it in flour.
1. Roll out the dough into a 5 inch diameter circle.
1. Spoon a large portion of the filling in the center of the dough .
1. Seal the edges by by bringing together the ends.
1. Dust the paratha lightly in flour again and roll gently to remove any air pockets.
1. Roll the paratha, adding very little pressure so the filling stays inside and does not ooze out.
1. Proceed the same way to fill the remaining Paneer and Green Peas Stuffed Paratha.
1. The next step is to cook the paratha on the skillet or roti tawa.
1. Preheat the skillet and then place the rolled out paratha on the skillet.
1. Flip and cook on both sides until you see brown spots appearing on the outside.
1. Drizzle ghee or oil on the paratha and cook on low to medium heat until the paratha is cooked through and golden brown on both sides.
1. Once cooked, transfer the paratha to a platter and cook the remaining Paneer and Green Peas Stuffed  Parathas the similar way.
1. Serve the Paneer and Green Peas Recipe along with Palak Raita or just a Aam Ka Chunda by the side to relish your for your breakfast meal and lunch.
