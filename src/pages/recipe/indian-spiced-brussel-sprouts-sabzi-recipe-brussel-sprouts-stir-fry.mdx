### Ingredients

- 1 Red Bell pepper (Capsicum) - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 teaspoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 cup Homemade tomato puree.
- 3 cloves Garlic - grated.
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1/4 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 inch Ginger - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 500 grams Brussel sprouts - quartered.
- 1/4 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- 5 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1/4 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Indian Spiced Brussel Sprouts Sabzi Recipe (Brussel Sprouts Stir Fry), first get all the ingredients ready and keep them by the side.
1. When you have things prepped and ready, cooking can become fun and easy.
1. Heat a teaspoon of oil in a large wok over medium heat.
1. Add the quartered brussel sprouts, sprinkle some salt and stir fry the brussel sprouts until it gets cooked through.
1. This will take about 5 minutes.
1. Once done, turn off the heat and transfer the brussel sprouts to another bowl.
1. In the same wok, add a teaspoon of oil over medium heat.
1. Add the mustard and cumin seeds and allow them to crackle.
1. Add the ginger, garlic, onions, bell pepper and saute until the onions and bell peppers are translucent and cooked through.
1. Add the tomato puree, along with the bay leaf and the remaining powders for the masala and saute them for a couple of minutes until the tomato puree comes to a bubble.
1. At this stage add the stir-fried brussel sprouts and toss them in the masala for a couple of minutes until it gets coated well in the masala.
1. Check the salt and spice levels and adjust to suit your taste.
1. Once done, turn off the heat, stir in the chopped coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve the Indian Spiced Brussel Sprouts Sabzi along with Phulka, Sindhi Kadhi and steamed rice for lunch or a weeknight dinner.
