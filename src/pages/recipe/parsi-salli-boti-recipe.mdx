### Ingredients

- 2 Onions - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 2 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 1/2 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- Sunflower Oil - as required.
- 2 Tomatoes - pureed.
- 4 Green Chillies - (adjustable).
- 800 grams Mutton.
- 2 tablespoons Red Chilli powder.
- 3 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1 cup Potato (Aloo) - fries.
- 1/2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- Salt - as required.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mace (Javitri).
- 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Garlic - paste.
- 2 tablespoons Ginger - paste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 4 Black pepper powder.
- 2 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 4 Cloves (Laung).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Parsi Salli Boti recipe, wash mutton pieces nicely under running water, strain and keep aside in a mixing bowl.
1. Add yogurt/curd along with ginger garlic paste, coriander powder, red chilli powder and turmeric.
1. Season with salt and mix well.
1. Add the curd mix to the mutton pieces and mix well such that everything incorporates well.
1. Cover the marinade with a cling wrap and keep the bowl in the refrigerator for around 30 to 40 minutes.
1. Heat oil in a thick bottomed pan or wok and add bay leaf.
1. Now add chopped onions and saute till they are golden in colour.
1. Add in the chopped tomatoes and mix well.
1. Cook till the tomatoes soften and turn mushy.
1. Take the mutton marinade and add in to the wok and ground spice mix.
1. Stir well and fry till the texture becomes a little watery.
1. Adjust salt if needed and also add more oil if required.
1. Close the lid of the allow the mutton to cook in medium to low flames till it softens.
1. This might take an hour or so.
1. Once the mutton is cooked, turn the flames high and fry nicely till oil starts leaving the sides.
1. Adjust the consistency of the gravy to semi liquid and give a boil again.
1. Turn off the heat and garnish with plenty of potato fried and coriander.
1. Serve Parsi Salli Boti along with Whole Wheat Lachha Paratha and Steamed Rice for a weekend lunch or dinner.
