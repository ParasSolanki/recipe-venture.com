### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Enos fruit salt.
- 1 cup Sweet corn.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - Chopped for garnishing.
- 1 cup Corn meal.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves - torn.
- 1 inch Ginger - sliced.
- 1/2 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt) - whisked.
- Sunflower Oil - for greasing the plates/moulds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2-3 Green Chillies - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Sugar.
- 1/2 teaspoon Lemon juice.
- 2 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Makai Dhokla, firstly crush the sweet corn in a blender along with green chilies and ginger.
1. In a mixing bowl add the crushed corn paste, corn dalia and sour curd.
1. Add asafoetida, turmeric powder, salt, sugar, lime juice and mix everything well.
1. Add water to make a thick, dropping consistency batter and keep it aside for 30 minutes.
1. Meanwhile, prepare the steamer and grease two 8 inch steel plates or any moulds of choice with a little oil.
1. Add the fruit salt to the Dhokla batter, mix well and immediately pour the batter into the prepared plates/moulds.
1. Steam for 12-15 minutes or till a knife/ tester inserted comes out clean.
1. The next step is tempering.
1. In a small pan heat the oil and add the mustard seeds and once they crackle, add the cumin seeds.
1. Add the curry leaves, asafoetida and red chilli powder and take the pan off the heat.
1. Drizzle this tempering (tadka) over the steamed dhokla using a spoon.
1. Serve Makai Dhokla with Dhaniya Pudina Chutney and hot Masala chai.
