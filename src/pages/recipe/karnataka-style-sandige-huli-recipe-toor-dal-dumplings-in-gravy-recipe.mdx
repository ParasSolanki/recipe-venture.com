### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 cloves Garlic - chopped.
- 1 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal).
- 1/2 cup Tamarind Water.
- 1 teaspoon Jaggery.
- 2 Green Chilli - chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 2 tablespoon Fresh coconut - grated.

### Instructions

1. To begin preparing Karnataka Style Sandige Huli Recipe, soak the dal in water for at least 3 hours and keep it.
1. In a grinder add the dal without water and green chili to a coarse paste.
1. Take more than half of the mixture and keep it aside in a bowl.
1. Add some more water into the mixer and grind the mixture to a smooth paste and reserve for later use.
1. To the coarsely ground dal mixture, add coconut grated, chopped coriander leaves hing, turmeric and mix well.
1. Heat a kadai with oil, add mustard seeds allow it splutter for 10 seconds.
1. Add the mixed toor dal mixture and keep saut√©ing for 2 minutes.
1. Switch off the heat, allow it to rest for some time and start shaping them into balls.
1. Heat a sauce pan with ghee, add mustard seeds and allow it to splutter for 10 seconds.
1. Add the smoothly ground dal mixture, with chopped coriander, jaggery, tamarind water, salt and turmeric powder.
1. You can add some more water to make the consistency thin.
1. Check for salt and add the dumplings into the dal mixture.
1. Allow it to boil for 10 minutes and switch off the heat.
1. To temper, heat a small tempering kadai with oil and add the urad dal, garlic and saute until they become light brown.
1. Add curry leaves and let it splutter and immediately pour it over the dal and serve.
1. Serve the Sandige Huli Recipe along with hot steamed rice, palya and Crispy Palak Pakora Recipe to complete your rich meal.
