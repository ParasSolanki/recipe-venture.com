### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Black pepper powder.
- 1 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 Potato (Aloo) - chopped into 1/2 inch cubes.
- 1 teaspoon Jaggery - powdered (optional).
- Salt - to taste.
- 12 sprig Dill leaves.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/4 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1/4 cup Raw Peanuts (Moongphali).
- 1 Green Chilli - chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 5 Curry leaves.
- 1/2 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal).
- 1 tablespoon White Urad Dal (Split).

### Instructions

1. To begin with Sabsige Soppu Kootu, wash and clean the leaves well and chop them finely.
1. Keep it aside.
1. Now wash and soak the toor dal in water for about 15 minutes.
1. Drain the water and put daal in a pressure cooker.
1. Add the chopped dill leaves, peanuts, chopped potato and add 2 cup of water.
1. Cook it till the cooker releases 3 whistles.
1. Switch off the heat and let it cool down.
1. In the meantime, take little oil in a pan and saute the urad dal with green chillies and pepper.
1. Once the urad dal  browns, turn off the flame and add grated coconut to this pan.
1. Once it cools, empty this mixture to a mixer jar and grind it into a smooth paste.
1. Once the cooker has cooled down, open the lid and stir in the contents.
1. Make sure that the dal is cooked.
1. Add in the ground coconut paste, salt, jaggery powder and a cup of water in the cooker on a low to medium flame and bring it to a boil.
1. The kootu would have to reach to a creamy consistency.
1. The next step is tempering.
1. For the process, heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds.
1. Let it crackle.
1. Now add the red chillies and curry leaves and pour it over the gravy.
1. Serve Sabsige Soppu Kootu with Chapatis or Steamed Rice, Carrot Poriyal  and Elai Vadam for a complete meal.
