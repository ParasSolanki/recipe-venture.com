### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Ghee.
- 1 cup All Purpose Flour (Maida).
- 1/4 teaspoon Salt.
- 1 cup Water.
- 2 tablespoons Chiroti Rava.
- 1 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 1/2 cup Sugar.
- 1/4 cup Lukewarm Water.

### Instructions

1. Mithi Mathri Recipe, first lets prepare the dough.
1. Mix the maida, sooji and salt together in a mixing bowl.
1. Next, add the ghee to the mixture and rub between your fingers until the dough turns crumbly and resembles breadcrumbs.
1. Add the warm water to it a little by little, while bringing it together into a dough.
1. Press together till it forms a ball.
1. Knead the dough for 3-4 minutes till it no longer sticks, and easily forms a ball.
1. Set it aside to rest for 5 minutes.
1. Then, knead it again till soft for about 5 minutes.
1. Cover the dough with a muslin cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
1. After 30 minutes, divide the dough in 16 equal parts.
1. Make balls and keep aside for 5 minutesNext, roll out the mathris by rolling each ball into a disc, about 1/4 inch thick and 2-3 inches in diameter.
1. Poke the rolled out mathri with fork or knife so that it doesn't fluff up while frying.
1. Warm some oil in a kadhai and deep fry the mathris for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn golden brown.
1. Remove from the oil using a slotted spoon and keep aside to cool.
1. Meanwhile, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling together water, sugar and cardamom powder in a heavy-bottomed saucepan.
1. Once the mixture comes to a boil, reduce the heat and continue to cook till the syrup reaches two-string consistency.
1. This should take about 8-10 minutes on low heat.
1. Switch off the heat and dip the pre-fried mathri in to the sugar syrup.
1. Keep the mathri sub-merged in sugar syrup for about 30 seconds so that it is completely coated.
1. Place the sugar-coated mathri on a tray till completely dry.
1. Then the Mithi Mathri store in an airtight container when completely dry and cool.
1. You can make this ahead and serve it at a festive gathering too.
