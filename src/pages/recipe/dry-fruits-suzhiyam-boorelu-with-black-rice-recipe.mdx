### Ingredients

- Water - as required.
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 cup Black Rice.
- 3/4 cup Mixed nuts - (almond.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 1 cup White Urad Dal (Split).
- Sunflower Oil - for deep fry.
- 1 cup Dry Fruits - (raisins.
- Ghee - Ghee as required.
- 1 cup Idli Rice.
- 1/2 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Dry Fruits Suzhiyam (Boorelu) With Black Rice recipe, we will first start preparing the batter.
1. Soak urad dal, black rice, idli rice for a minimum of 4 to 5 hours or overnight.
1. Grind the urad dal in a mixer until it becomes like a butter consistency.
1. Add idli rice, black rice, salt and water to the urad dal mixture.
1. Make sure it is ground like an idli consistency.
1. Leave it for 4 to 6 hours for fermentation.
1. This step is optional for this dish.
1. If you ferment you may use the remaining batter for making dosa.
1. Once the grinding is complete, add rava and make the batter thick enough to dip the dry fruit balls.
1. The next step is to make the stuffing.
1. Dry roast the nuts until the raw smell leaves and grind it in a mixer.
1. Add ghee, dry fruits and give it a good mix.
1. After 3 minutes, add elachi and cook until they form a sticky consistency.
1. Sprinkle water if needed.
1. Water is surely needed if you are using raisins.
1. Lastly add the powdered nuts and mix it well.
1. Switch off the gas.
1. Grease ghee in your palms and make the mix as round balls.
1. This needs to be done when the mix is hot else we cannot bind them together.
1. Meanwhile, heat oil in a deep fry pan.
1. Dip the balls made out of nuts in the batter and fry until it is cooked well.
1. Take out and put them in a tissue paper to absorb the excess oil.
1. Suzhiyam or Boorelu is ready to be served.
1. Serve Dry Fruits Suzhiyam (Boorelu) With Black Rice during festivals along with other traditional sweets such as Rava Kesari Bhath and Paruppu Payasam.
