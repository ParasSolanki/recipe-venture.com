### Ingredients

- 2 cloves Garlic.
- 2 cups Green Moong Sprouts.
- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 2 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Jaggery.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 Lemon - Juice squeezed.
- 3 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 cup Fresh Pomegranate Fruit Kernels.
- 1/4 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1/4 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 Green Chilli - roughly chopped.
- 1 inch Ginger - roughly chopped.
- 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - Juice from one lemon.
- 1 cup Mint Leaves (Pudina).
- 2 sprig Mint Leaves (Pudina) - Salt to taste.
- Chaat Masala Powder - for sprinkling.
- 2 tablespoons Badam (Almond).
- 2 Green Chillies - finely chopped.
- 2 sprig Curry leaves - finely chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 4 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the moong sprout chaat, first prepare all the ingredients and keep them handy.
1. For the moong sprouts, heat a teaspoon of oil Into a preheated pan; add mustard seeds, cumin seeds and allow it to crackled.
1. Once crackled, add ginger, curry leaves, green chillies and turmeric powder.
1. Give it a stir for a few secondsAdd the sprouted green moong dal, salt to taste and 2 to 3 tablespoons of water.
1. Cover the pan and allow the green moong sprouts to get softened just a bit.
1. You want the sprouts to get soft, yet hold its shape and not become mushy.
1. Once done, squeeze the juice from one lemon, stir in chopped coriander leaves and keep aside.
1. For the pudina chutney, into a blender add the mint leaves, green chillies, ginger, garlic, almonds, salt, jaggery and juice from one lemon.
1. Add 1/4 cup of water and blend to make a smooth pudina chutney.
1. Keep aside.
1. To assemble the chaat, spoon 1/2 cup of moong sprouts into individual serving bowls, drizzle the pudina chutney, garnish with onions, tomatoes, coriander leaves & finally sprinkle a little chaat masala on the top & serve immediately.
1. Serve Sprouted Moong Chaat as a tea time snack with hot Masala Chai or for your next Chaat potluck party with Spicy & Tangy Pakodi Chaat.
