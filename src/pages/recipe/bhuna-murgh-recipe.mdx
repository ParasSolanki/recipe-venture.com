### Ingredients

- 4 Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 tablespoon Ghee - or oil.
- 2 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 750 grams Chicken.
- 1 tablespoon Cashew nuts - paste (optional).
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 4 Green Chillies - slit.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 3 Tomato - finely chopped or pureed.
- 1 tablespoon Lemon juice - adjustable.
- 2 inch Ginger - minced.
- 2 Onion - finely chopped.
- 8 cloves Garlic - roughly pounded.
- 1 teaspoon Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves).
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1 Star anise.
- 4 Cloves (Laung).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Bhuna Murgh recipe, wash and clean the chicken pieces in water and apply salt and lemon juice all over on chicken pieces as a marinade.
1. Cover the bowl of marinated chicken with a cling wrap and set in refrigerator.
1. Heat ghee or oil  in a non-stick pan and add bay leaf, clove, cumin seeds, green and black cardamoms, cinnamon, pepper corns and aniseed and wait till they crackle.
1. Add finely chopped onion and keep stirring.
1. Add green chillies, minced ginger and pounded garlic and saute till oil begins to separate.
1. Now add fine chopped tomatoes or pureed tomatoes and season them with turmeric, coriander powder, red chilli powder and garam masala powder.
1. Simmer the flame and cover the pan for 3-4 minutes.
1. Take off the lid and keep stirring until the onion-tomato mixture softened and seems like a bhuna paste.
1. Now add chicken pieces and saute well with bhuna mixture.
1. Add 2 cups of water, cover the pan, simmer the flame and leave to cook until chicken is well done.
1. Once the chicken is nicely cooked and tender and the bhuna masala has started to thicken, add crushed kasuri methi and cashew paste (optional).
1. Mix well, and cover the lid to cook for another 5-6 minutes.
1. Garnish the dish with fine chopped coriander leaves.
1. Serve Bhuna Murgh along with Garlic Naan for a weekend night dinner.
