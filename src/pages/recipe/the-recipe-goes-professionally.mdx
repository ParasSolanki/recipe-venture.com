### Ingredients

- 1 Green Chilli - finely chopped.
- 1 cup Kabuli Chana (White Chickpeas).
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Anardana Powder (Pomegranate Seed Powder).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 inch Ginger - grated.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 2 cloves Garlic - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1/2 tablespoon Lemon juice.
- Salt - as per taste.
- 2 Tomatoes - finely chopped.
- 2 Tea bags.
- Sunflower Oil - as required.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Peshawari Chole Recipe; first wash and soak chickpeas in water for overnight or at least 6 to 8 hours.
1. Next day, drain the water from the soaked chickpeas in a colander and transfer to Pressure Cooker.
1. Add 3 Cups of water in Pressure Cooker with cardamom, cinnamon, salt and stir once.
1. Arrange tea bags over the chickpeas and cover the cooker with its weight on.
1. Allow the chole to pressure cook for about 4 to 5 whistles.
1. After about 4 to 5 whistles, turn the heat to low and simmer the chole for another 15 minutes or until the chole are completely cooked.
1. Turn off heat after 15 minutes and allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. While chickpeas are in Pressure cooker, we will prepare the masala for the Peshawari Chole.
1. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
1. Add the chopped onions and saute till they turn light brown in color.
1. Once onion turn brown add chopped tomato, green chili, ginger, garlic and saute for 5 minutes.
1. Add the chili powder, coriander powder, pomegranate powder and garam masala.
1. Stir and fry the masala till tomatoes turn soft and masala leaves oil.
1. Once steam is released open the lid of the pressure cooker.
1. Discard the tea bags.
1. Reserve 1 Cup of boiled chickpea water.
1. Add the chickpeas along with whole spices from the cooker to the masala.
1.  Stir well and season with some more salt if required and add a small quantity of reserved water to the chickpeas.
1. Cover the pan with the lid and let chickpeas simmer in the masala for 10 - 15 minutes over medium heat.
1. Open the lid and turn off the heat.
1. Transfer Peshawari Chole to serving bowl drizzle lemon juice just before serving and garnish with some chopped coriander.
1. Serve the Peshawari Chole along with hot Bhatura or Naan, it makes an ideal Sunday lunch for the whole family.
1. You can also top Samosa or Aloo Tikkis with the tasty Peshawari Chole and relish it as a tea time snack.
