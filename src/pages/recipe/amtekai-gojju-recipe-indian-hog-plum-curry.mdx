### Ingredients

- 4 to 5 Hog Plum (Amtekai).
- 1 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/4 cup Jaggery.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 4 to 5 Dry Red Chillies.
- 3 tablespoons Coconut Oil.
- 1/4 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 2 teaspoons Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 2 teaspoons White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Amtekai Gojju Recipe (Indian Hog Plum Curry), first get all the ingredients prepped and ready.
1. Wash and lightly scrape the skin of the amtekai.
1. Cut amtekai to pieces and don't discard the seed.
1. Place amtekai (hog plum) along with seed in a saucepan with a cup of water and jaggery and bring it to a brisk boil.
1. Once it comes to a boil, turn the heat to low and simmer until the amtekai (hog plum) is soft.
1. The pit (Seed) will be hard & is not edible but after cooking, it absorbs in all the flavors and spices.
1. It 's a wonderful treat to suck its juice.
1. Meanwhile in a small pan add few drops of oil.
1. Add all the ingredients mentioned under masala except coconut and sesame.
1. Roast them in medium low flame till red chillies becomes crisp and dal turns golden color.
1. Add coconut to that and just saute for about minute.
1. Once done, transfer all the ingredients to a mixer jar.
1. Next,dry roast sesame seeds in the same pan till it turns golden.
1. Add the sesame seeds to the mixer jar containing other roasted masala ingredients.
1. Once it's little cooled, add little water and make a very smooth paste.
1. Add gojju masala ground paste & salt to cooked amtekai (hog plum) and mix well.
1. Cook this in a medium flame till the amtekai gojju starts boiling.
1. Turn the heat to low and add a tablespoon of raw coconut oil and allow it to boil for another 8-10 mins.
1. Stir in between and allow the amtekai gojju to thicken.
1. Check the salt and spices and adjust to suit your taste and turn off the heat.
1. Transfer the gojju to a serving bowl.
1. The final step is to make the seasoning.
1. Heat oil in a small pan over medium heat; add the mustard and allow it to crackle.
1. Add the curry leaves and asafoetida and stir for a few seconds and add this seasoning to the Amtekai Gojju.
1. Serve the Amtekai Gojju along with steamed rice topped with ghee for a delicious lunch or a weeknight dinner or even for breakfast along with idlis or dosas.
