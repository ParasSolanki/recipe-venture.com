### Ingredients

- 2 teaspoons Ginger Garlic Paste.
- Salt - as per need.
- 1/2 cup Button mushrooms - diced (canned or store-bought fresh mushrooms).
- 1/4 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 cups Green peas (Matar) - boiled.
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves).
- Sunflower Oil - to cook.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 tablespoons Fresh cream.
- 1/2 teaspoon Tomato Ketchup - or sugar can be used.
- 1/4 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 Tomato - pureed.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.

### Instructions

1. To begin Matar Mushroom Curry, heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
1. After they splutter, add the chopped onions and fry them till light brown on medium heat.
1. Once done add ginger garlic paste and saut√© it till cooked.
1. After this add tomato puree and salt and cook for 2 minutes until combined.
1. Then add all the spices like the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chilli powder and garam masala and saute for about 2-3 minutes.
1. Next add the green peas and mushrooms and mix them well.
1. Cook for another 5-6 minutes till water from the mushrooms have evaporated.
1. Once done add kasoori methi and some water to cook till the peas are cooked.
1. Once the peas are cooked add fresh cream and mix well to combine.
1. Turn off the heat.
1. Transfer the Matar Mushroom Curry into a serving bowl garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve Matar Mushroom Curry with whole wheat phulkas, tawa paratha or any other flatbread like Besan & Atta Ki Roti.
