### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Ghee.
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 2-1/2 cup Vivatta Maida - or use whole wheat flour.
- 1 teaspoon Cinnamon Powder (Dalchini).
- 4 tablespoons Sugar - powdered.
- 1 cup Water.
- 1/4 cup Sugar.
- 1/4 teaspoon Salt - to taste.

### Instructions

1. To begin with the Shankarpali recipe, first heat the water, sugar in a saucepan until the sugar dissolves.
1. Take off the heat and cool it slightly.
1. Sieve the flour and salt into a mixing bowl and mix it along with ghee, till it forms coarse crumbs.
1. Gradually add the water-sugar mixture into the flour.
1. Mix and knead the flour into a firm but pliable dough.
1. Keep it covered in a cool place for 30 minutes.
1. Heat oil in a wok and divide the dough into 3-4 portions.
1. Roll out each portion into a thin (about 7-8 inches) circle or depending on the thickness you want.
1. Cut squares or diamonds using a pizza cutter, knife or a special Shankarpali cutter.
1. Deep fry on low-medium flame in a deep fry pan till the Shankarpali turn golden and crisp.
1. Mix the cinnamon and powder sugar in a small bowl.
1. While the Shankarpali is still hot, dust it with the cinnamon sugar.
1. Cool completely and store in an airtight container.
1. Serve Shakkarpara as a tea time snack or you can even pack these in your kids snack box.
