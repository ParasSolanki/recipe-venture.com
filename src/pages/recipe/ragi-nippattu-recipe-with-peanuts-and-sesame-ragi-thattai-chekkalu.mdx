### Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 1/4 cup Roasted Peanuts (Moongphali) - coarsely pounded.
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 cups Ragi Flour (Finger Millet/ Nagli).
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil - hot for the dough.
- 1 tablespoon Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 1/4 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal) - soaked in hot water for 30 minutes.
- 1/4 cup Rice flour.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Ragi Nippattu Recipe with Peanuts and Sesame, soak the chana dal in hot water for about 30 minutes.
1. After 30 minutes drain the water out.
1. In a mixing bowl, add the ragi flour, rice flour, crushed peanuts, salt, red chilli powder, asafoetida, soaked chana dal, sesame seeds, hot oil and mix well.
1. The mixture should resemble coarse crumbs.
1. Add little warm water at a time and knead to make a firm dough.
1. Cover the dough and allow it to rest for 10 minutes.
1. Heat a pan with oil for deep frying the Ragi Nippattu Recipe.
1. Grease your fingers and palms with oil.
1. Make marble sized portions of the Ragi Nippattu dough.
1. Place the dough on a greased surface and flatten the dough with your fingers and shaping it in the form of a disc of about 1/4 inch thick or thinner.
1. Gradually slide a few Ragi Nippattu discs into the preheated oil and deep fry on medium heat until crisp and you notice light brown spots on the ragi.
1. Since Ragi is dark, it wil be hard to notice these, but a good way to know if it is done is to tap the Ragi Nippattu with a spoon and it should feel stiff and firm.
1. Once done, drain the excess oil from the Ragi Nippattu and place it on paper towels and allow it to cool.
1. Store the Ragi Nippattu/ Ragi Thattai / Chekkalu in an air tight container.
1. Serve the Ragi Nippattu/ Ragi Thattai / Chekkalu along with a hot cup of filter coffee for a evening tea time snack.
