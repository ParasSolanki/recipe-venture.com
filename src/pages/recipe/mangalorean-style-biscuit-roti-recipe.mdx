### Ingredients

- 1 pinch Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1 tablespoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 sprig Curry leaves - finely chopped..
- 1 tablespoon Sambar Powder.
- 1 teaspoon Baking powder.
- 1/2 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Salt - to taste.
- 3 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 2 tablespoons Sunflower Oil - hot.
- 1/4 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/4 cup Water.
- 1/2 tablespoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1/2 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).
- 3 tablespoons Sugar.
- 3/4 cup Whole Wheat Flour.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Mangalorean Style Biscuit Roti Recipe we will first mix the dough.
1. Take a mixing bowl and add the flour, baking powder and salt.
1. Mix well.
1. Add in the hot oil and little water and knead well to form a firm dough.
1. The point of adding hot oil to knead the dough is to make the biscuit roti crispier when fried.
1. Cover and keep aside til you make the filling.
1. Into a preheated pan add oil.
1. Once the oil is hot add the mustard seeds and curry leaves and let them splutter.
1. Once done add the urad dal and roast till the dal becomes slightly brown in colour.
1. Once done add all the spice powders like the cumin powder, coriander powder, sambar powder, red chili powder turmeric powder, semolina salt and sugar.
1. Saute on low flame for 3 to 4 minutes until well combined.
1. Keep this aside.
1. To make the RotisPinch out a small lemon sized dough from the dough.
1.  Roll it out flat by dusting some flour on your kitchen surface.
1. Take a tablespoon of the filling and place it at the center of the rolled out dough.
1. Start bringing the sides of dough to the center.
1. Seal it completely otherwise if there are any holes it will start to absorb oil.
1. Dust some more flour and flatten the biscuit roti to 2 inch diameter.
1. Let the thickness of the dough be almost Â½ a centimeter.
1. Repeat the same process for the rest of the dough.
1. Heat a kadi with enough oil and keep it at a medium heat.
1. Make sure it does not heat up too much.
1. Now start to fry these Mangalorean Style Biscuit roti one by one till it is golden brown on either side.
1. Drain it on a kitchen napkin and serve.
1. Serve the Mangalorean Style Biscuit Roti Recipe as a Tea Time snack with Adrak Chai and enjoy your evening time.
