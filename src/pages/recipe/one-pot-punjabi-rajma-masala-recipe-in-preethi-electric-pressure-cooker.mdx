### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - a small bunch.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera) - roasted.
- 2 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Butter.
- 1/2 cup Homemade tomato puree.
- 1 cup Rajma (Large Kidney Beans).
- 2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 Onion - roughly chopped.
- 1 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 4 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Rajma Masala recipe, we will first soak the rajma immersed completely in 3 cups of water for about 8 to 10 hours.
1. To make the Rajma Masala turn on the Preethi Pressure Cooker.
1. Change the setting on the pressure cooker to the saute mode.
1. Heat a teaspoon of oil in the electric pressure cooker pan; add the onion, garlic, ginger and saute for 3 to 4 minutes until the onions start to turn golden in colour.
1. Once done stir in the tomato puree, the turmeric powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, cardamom pods and cinnamon stick.
1. Saute for another couple of minutes and then add in the soaked rajma along with its water.
1. Add any additional water if required.
1. There should enough water such that the water level is at least 1 inch above the rajma.
1. Cover the pressure cooker, set the mode to rajma mode and wait until done.
1. Once done the electric pressure cooker will go into the keep warm mode.
1. Allow the pressure cooker to release its pressure naturally - you will notice the pressure indicator has gone down.
1. The rajma will continue to cook in the pressure cooker as long as there is pressure inside.
1. Once the pressure has released, open the cooker.
1. The rajma should be now cooked completely.
1. If you press the rajma between your fingers it will get mashed easily.
1. Once the Rajma Masala is cooked, check the salt and spice levels and adjust to suit your taste.
1. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve the Rajma Masala dish for Sunday Lunch along with Steamed Hot Rice and Aloo Parwal Sabzi making the meal wholesome and nutritious.
