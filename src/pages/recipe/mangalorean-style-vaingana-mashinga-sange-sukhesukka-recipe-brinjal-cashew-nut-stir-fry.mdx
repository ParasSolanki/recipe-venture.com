### Ingredients

- 4 Dry Red Chillies.
- 4 cloves Garlic - crushed.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 Onion - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 6 Small Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - cut into wedges.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1 tablespoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 tablespoon White Urad Dal (Split).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Mangalorean Style Vaingana Mashinga Sange Sukhe by first dry roasting the ingredients (urad dal, chana dal, coriander seeds, whole peppercorns, dry red chillies) on a flat skillet over a medium heat.
1. Roast them until it is golden brown, then cool it down and then grind them into a smooth powder and keep it aside.
1. Heat a flat skillet on a medium heat, add oil and temper the mustard seeds and curry leaves for few seconds.
1. Then add in the chopped onions and garlic and saute till the onions turn translucent.
1. Now add the eggplant along with the ground masala, turmeric powder and red chilli powder.
1. Give it a toss and add the cashew nuts as well and mix well.
1. Sprinkle some water and salt so that the egg plants gets cooked well, cover it with a lid and cook the brinjal.
1. Keep saut√©ing and turning over so that the eggplant does not get stuck at the bottom of the pan.
1. Once done serve it by sprinkling some chopped coriander leaves over it.
1. Serve the Mangalorean Style Vaingana Mashinga Sange Sukhe/Sukka Recipe along with Mangalorean Padengi Ghassi, Goli Baje Recipe and Steamed Rice for your weekend Lunch.
