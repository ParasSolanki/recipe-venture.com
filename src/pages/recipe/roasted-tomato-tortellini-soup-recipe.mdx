### Ingredients

- Parmesan cheese - shavings as required.
- 3 tablespoons Ricotta Cheese - (or use paneer).
- Salt - to taste.
- 4-5 tablespoons Extra Virgin Olive Oil.
- 2 cloves Garlic - minced.
- 3 cloves Garlic - for roasting.
- 2 Onions - sliced.
- 1 pinch Sugar.
- 6 Tomatoes.
- Fresh Herbs - handful.
- 2 teaspoons Black pepper powder - (adjust).
- Vegetable stock.
- 3/4 cup Whole Wheat Flour.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Roasted Tomato Tortellini soup, first roast the tomatoes.
1. To fire roast the tomatoesStove top method: Place them directly on a gas burner or on a fine wire mesh placed on the burner and roast till the skin chars.
1. Cool and peel off the skin.
1. Oven method: Quarter and toss them in 1-2 tablespoons of olive oil.
1. Place on a parchment lined baking sheet and bake in a pre-heated oven at 200 degree Celsius for about 20 minutes or till they just start browning.
1. Soup:Toss the onion and 2-3 cloves of garlic with 1 tablespoon (or as required) olive oil and salt.
1. Roast/bake in the oven till it just browns.
1. Blend together the tomatoes, onions and garlic  in a food processor or mixer.
1. Sieve through a mesh to remove skin (if roasted in the oven) and seeds.
1. To make the Tortellini filling, mix the herbs, minced garlic, black pepper, Ricotta in a small mixing bowl.
1. Keep covered till requiredTo make the Tortellini, add salt (as required) to the whole wheat flour and place it in a mixing bowl.
1. Add water and 2 teaspoons of olive oil to make a stiff but pliable dough.
1. Cover and keep aside for 30 minutes.
1. Dust a working surface with flour.
1. Roll out the dough into a very thin circle.
1. Cut the circle into rounds using a 3-inch round cutter.
1. Space the rounds as close together as possible.
1. Gather the scraps and reuse them.
1. Place a teaspoon of the Ricotta filling at the center of each round.
1. Run a finger dipped in water along the edge of the pasta round to moisten it.
1. Fold over the dough to seal and make a half moon shape.
1. Draw the ends together to form the typical Tortellini shape.
1. Heat water in a saucepan.
1. Add salt.
1. Once the water boils carefully drop the Tortellini in it (use a slotted spoon if required).
1. Cook for 2-3 minutes or till the Tortellini float on the surface.
1. Take them out using a slotted spoon.
1. Heat the Tomato â€“ onion-garlic puree in a saucepan.
1. Add salt, pepper and pinch of sugar.
1. Add enough vegetable stock to make a thick soup.
1. Bring it to a boil and carefully drop the cooked Tortellini in it.
1. Boil for a few seconds more.
1. Serve Roasted Tomato Tortellini soup garnished with Parmesan cheese and herbs, along with garlic bread or Grilled Winter Vegetable Salad Recipe for a quick weeknight meal.
