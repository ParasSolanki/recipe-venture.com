### Ingredients

- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 4 teaspoons Fresh coconut - finely chopped.
- 1 Cauliflower (gobi) - roughly chopped into smaller pieces.
- 1 Cloves (Laung).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Ginger - grated.
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 3 to 4 Green Chillies - cut lengthwise.
- 1 teaspoon Poppy seeds.
- 4 cloves Garlic - grated.
- 1 tablespoon Coconut Oil.
- 1/4 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Cashew nuts - approximately 6-8.
- 15 to 20 Shallots - finely chopped.
- 1 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 teaspoon Roasted Gram Dal (Pottukadalai).
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1 Star anise.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Cauliflower Kurma recipe, soak the ingredients given under 'masala paste' in 1/4 cup of warm water for 20 minutes and then grind them to a fine paste using little water in a grinder.
1. Bring 3 cups of water to a boil.
1. Add the chopped cauliflower and cook them for 5 minutes and switch off the stove.
1. Drain the water and set aside the cauliflower.
1. Heat a wide pan or wok over low flame with coconut oil.
1. Add all the ingredients mentioned under ‘for tempering’.
1. Now add the grated ginger, garlic, chopped shallots, tomatoes and a pinch of salt.
1. Keep stirring till the onions turn transparent and the tomatoes mushy.
1. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder and mix well.
1. Add 1/2 cup of water and let it simmer for 3-4 minutes.
1. Now, add the boiled cauliflower and allow it to simmer for 5 minutes.
1. When the cauliflower is completely soft boiled, add the ground masala paste and salt.
1. Mix well and cook over low flame for another 5 minutes only.
1. Switch off and garnish.
1. Cauliflower Kurma is a tasty, healthy and popular side dish served along with appam, idiyappam, phulka or malabar paratha.
