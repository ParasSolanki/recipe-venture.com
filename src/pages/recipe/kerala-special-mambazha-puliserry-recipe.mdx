### Ingredients

- 1 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt) - whisked.
- 4 Mango (Ripe).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 Green Chillies - sliced lengthwise.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 teaspoon Coconut Oil.
- 1/4 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 Dry Red Chilli - cut into halves.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Kerala Special Mambazha Puliserry recipe, first heat a heavy bottomed pan with 2 cups of water.
1. Add the mangoes, green chillies and cook covered for 10 to 15 minutes over low flame.
1. You can optionally squeeze out some pulp to make a gravy.
1. When the mangoes are simmering, prepare the masala paste by grinding coconut, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, chilli powder in a mixer jar using 1/4 cup of warm water to a smooth paste.
1. Once the mangoes are cooked, add the ground masala paste and cook the Mambazha Puliserry over low flame for 5 to 7 minutes in the same pan.
1. Now add the whisked curd and give the Mambazha Puliserry a brisk boil and turn off the heat.
1. Check the salt and spices and adjust accordingly.
1. Heat a tadka pan with coconut oil over medium heat; add the mustard seeds, methi seeds and allow them to crackle.
1. Add the dry red chillies and the curry leaves and stir fry for a few seconds.
1. Pour the tempering over the Mambazha Puliserry and transfer to a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. Serve Kerala Special Mambazha Puliserry with hot Steamed Rice and Elai Vadam for a delicious weekday meal.
