### Ingredients

- 5-6 Curry leaves - torn with hands.
- Mint Leaves (Pudina) - a few sprigs chopped.
- 1 Carrot (Gajjar) - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 inch Ginger - grated.
- 4 Garlic.
- 1 teaspoon Lemon juice.
- 1/2 cup Green Moong Dal (Whole) - lightly roasted and soaked in water for 30 minutes.
- 1 cup Broken Wheat (Dalia/ Godumai Rava) - lightly roasted.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1/4 cup Green beans (French Beans) - finely chopped.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 3-4 Green Chillies.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Broken Wheat And Green Moong Khichdi, first crush together the garlic cloves, cumin seeds and green chilies in a small mortar and pestle.
1. Keep it aside.
1. Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker and add mustards seeds.
1. Once they crackle, add the crushed garlic-cumin-chillies paste and the curry leaves.
1. Fry for a few seconds and then add the onion.
1. Saute for a minute and then add turmeric powder, asafoetida and ginger.
1. Mix it properly and add all the vegetables.
1. Saute for another minute and then add the dalia and green moong.
1. Add enough water (depending on the consistency required) and mix well.
1. About 3 cups for a porridge like consistency.
1. Sprinkle some salt and cook it till the cooker releases 2 whistles or till well cooked.
1. Wait for the pressure to come down on its own.
1. Garnish the khichdi with chopped mint and coriander, lime juice (if using) and serve immediately.
1. Serve Broken Wheat And Green Moong Khichdi with Mooli raita or Gujarati Kadhi and papad.
