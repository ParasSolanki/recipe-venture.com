### Ingredients

- 2 Green Chillies.
- Water - as required.
- 12 Cashew nuts.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2 tablespoons Dry coconut (kopra).
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 150 grams Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 1/8 cup Mint Leaves (Pudina).
- 3 Cloves (Laung).
- 2-1/2 cups Water.
- 1/4 cup Coriander (Dhania) Leaves.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 2 tablespoons Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1 Onion.
- 300 grams Mutton.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Hyderabadi Marag, add all the ingredients listed under ‘for pressure cook’ in a cooker.
1. Pressure cook for 1 whistle on high and and 3 whistles on medium flame.
1. Allow the cooker to release pressure on it self.
1. Meanwhile, roast and grind cashews and coconut to a fine paste in a blender.
1. Once the pressure is released add garam masala powder, coriander powder, cashew-coconut paste and saute it for about a minute.
1. Now add whisked yogurt and saute until the gravy thickens and oil separates from the sides of the cooker.
1. Add water, coriander leaves, mint leaves and simmer for 4-5 minutes more.
1. After 4 to 5 minutes, switch off the flame and it is ready to be served.
1. Serve Hyderabadi Marag with Rumali Rotis and phulkas for a weekend lunch or dinner.
