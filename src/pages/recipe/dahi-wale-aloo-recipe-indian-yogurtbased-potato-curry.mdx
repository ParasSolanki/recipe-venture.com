### Ingredients

- 1-1/2 cups Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 1/2 Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Garlic - finely chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1/3 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 4 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ginger - finely chopped.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Gram flour (besan).
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 2 Green Chillies - chopped.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped or garnish.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 4 Potatoes (Aloo) - chopped.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Dahi Wale Aloo Recipe, first wash the potatoes thoroughly.
1. Then pressure cook them till they are cooked soft, but not completely mushy.
1. You can follow this method to learn how to pressure cook them.
1. Once the pressure has released from the cooker, open the line, drain the water and allow the potatoes to cool.
1. Then, peel them and roughly break into chunks.
1. In a large bowl, add curd and besan with a little water and whisk them together to make a smooth batter.
1. There should not be any lumps in the curds.
1. Whisk till it is smooth and then set aside.
1. In a wok or kadhai, warm some oil.
1. Add cumin seeds and hing.
1. As soon as cumin begins to crackle, add finely chopped onion and saute till the onions soften.
1. Next add the finely chopped ginger, garlic and green chilies along with the curry leaves and toss them all together for a couple of minutes.
1. Add the red chilli powder, coriander powder and turmeric and saute the masala well until it is cooked through and begins to release oil from the sides.
1. If you find the masala sticking at this stage, add a little water (2 tablespoons) in it and toss well.
1. Add the potatoes to the masala and mix again till the potatoes are well-coated.
1. Turn the heat to low and start pouring the beaten curd over the potatoes.
1. Mix well.
1. Adjust salt as per your taste and close the lid to allow the curry to simmer for 5 minutes.
1. Once done, turn the heat off, garnish with coriander leaves and serve with Steamed Rice or Phulkas.
