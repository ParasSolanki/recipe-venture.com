### Ingredients

- Salt - as required.
- 1 tablespoon Lemon juice.
- 1 teaspoon Dessicated Coconut.
- 20 grams Tamarind.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 5 Dry Red Chillies - broken.
- 1/2 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Mysore Chutney Recipe, first Soak dry red chillies in warm water for half an hour.
1. Soak the tamarind in little water to extract the pulp.
1. Heat oil in a wide pan.
1. Add chana dal and roast well on low to medium heat until golden brown in colour.
1. Once done, keep it aside to cool.
1. Once cooled grind together roasted dal, dry red chillies,, tamarind pulp, salt, coconut, curry leaves, ginger and water in a blender.
1. Grind to a coarse paste.
1. Transfer the Mysore chutney into a serving bowl and serve.
1. Serve Mysore Chutney with homemade soft idli or masala dosa and  South Indian Filter Coffee.
