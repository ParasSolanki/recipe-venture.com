### Ingredients

- Salt - as required.
- 4 tablespoons Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).
- 1/2 teaspoon Garlic - minced.
- 4 cups Buttermilk.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 2 tablespoons Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- 2 cups Poha (Flattened rice).
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 2 Green Chillies - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ginger - grated.
- 1 tablespoon Rice flour.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Poha Buttermilk Idli, whisk the buttermilk in a mixing bowl and keep it aside.
1. The next step is to temper the buttermilk.
1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan and add mustard seeds and curry leaves.
1. As soon as the mustard seeds splutters turn off the heat and  temper the buttermilk.
1. Now add poha, suji, rice atta and salt to the buttermilk.
1. Mix well to combine.
1. The batter may look thin but it will get thick after few hours.
1. Add chopped coriander, green chillies and grated ginger garlic to the poha idli batter and mix well.
1. Cover the bowl with a lid and let it rest overnight or minimum 4 hours.
1. Once the batter is done, stir and mix it vigorously with a ladle or spatula.
1. Grease the idli molds and put batter generously in each cavity.
1. Steam the idlis in a steamer till it turns fluffy and properly cooked.
1. the poha idlis will take about 8-10 minutes to steam.
1. Allow to rest for a few minutes and then unmould the poha idli into a serving plate and serve hot.
1. Serve Poha Buttermilk Idli with South Indian Coconut Chutney and Red Chilli Coconut Chutney for a wholesome breakfast.
