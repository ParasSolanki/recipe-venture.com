### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/4 cup Fresh coconut.
- 3 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 1-1/2 cups Black Eyed Beans (Lobia) - soaked.
- 1/4 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 2 Green Chillies - finely chopped.
- 2 sprig Curry leaves - finely chopped.
- 2 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 2 Dry Red Chilli - Salt to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Coconut Oil.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 4 or 5 Curry leaves - Juice from 1/2 lemon.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Karamani Sundal Recipe - Spicy Tossed Black Eyed Beans Recipe soak the black eyed beans for at least for 3 hours.
1. Into a pressure cooker, add the soaked black eyed beans,  salt to taste and add about 2 cups of water (including the soaked water).
1. Pressure cook for about 3 to 4 whistles, turn the heat to low and simmer for another 5 to 10 minutes.
1. In total about 20 minutes of cooking time.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. The beans should be soft, yet firm & not mushy for the sundal.
1. Drain the excess water and you can use it as a stock for dal/soups.
1. Heat oil into a preheated, add mustard and cumin seeds and allow it to crackle.
1. Once crackled, add the asafoetida, ginger, green chillies, curry leaves and saute for a few seconds.
1. Add turmeric powder, cooked black eyed beans and salt to taste.
1. Stir fry for 2 to 3 minutes and turn off the heat.
1. Squeeze juice from 1/2 lemon, add the coconut and coriander leaves and give it a stir.
1. Serve Karamani Sundal hot with Masala Chai  or South Indian Filter Coffee Recipe With Filter Coffee Powder as a healthy tea time snack for the family or make it for special festivals like Navratri.
