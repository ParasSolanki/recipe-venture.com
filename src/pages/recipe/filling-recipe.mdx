### Ingredients

- Cotton threads - to tie and secure the guards.
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 5 Karela (Bitter Gourd/ Pavakkai).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Mango pickle.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 teaspoon Salt.

### Instructions

1. To begin with Bharwa Karela, firstly peel the Karelas and rinse it off with salt and turmeric to remove the bitterness and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
1. Now make a vertical cut on each Karela to fill the spicy masala.
1. The next step is to prepare the masala.
1. Take a mixing bowl and add turmeric powder, red chilli powder, cumin seeds, coriander powder, mango pickle, onion and salt.
1. Mix it well.
1. Now fill the dried Karelas with this tangy and spicy filling.
1. After which wrap the Karela using a a cotton thread and secure it well by closing the slit.
1. Heat the oil in a kadhai and once the oil is hot enough, drop the stuffed Karelas into the oil carefully.
1. Fry them till they become brown.
1. Once it is done, remove the thread and place it on a tissue paper to remove the excess oil.
1. Serve Bharwa Karela along with Punjabi Dal Tadka and Boondi Raita for an everyday meal.
