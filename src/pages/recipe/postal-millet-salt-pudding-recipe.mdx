### Ingredients

- Curry leaves - handful.
- Salt - as required.
- 1 teaspoon Gingelly oil.
- 1 cup Foxtail Millet.
- 1/4 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 2 Green Chillies - finely chopped.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 cup Water.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - handful.
- 1/4 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Foxtail Millet Uppu Kozhukattai Recipe, soak foxtail millet in water for about 4 hours or overnight.
1. Drain the excess water and add the foxtail millet along with red chillies to a mixer grinder.
1. Add a cup of water and grind to a coarse paste.
1. Ensure not to add more water as it will make battery watery.
1. The water amount has to be added accurately as adding more water make it watery and makes the dumplings gummy.
1. At the same time, adding less amount of water makes the batter thick and makes hard dumplings.
1. Keep the batter aside.
1. In a medium Kadai, add oil and heat it over medium heat.
1. Once the oil is hot, add mustard seeds and urad dal.
1. Let it splutter.
1. Once done add curry leaves, coriander leaves, and chopped chilies.
1. Saute for a few seconds and add onions and saute until they become transparent.
1. Once the onions have turned translucent reduce the heat and add grounded batter to the pan.
1. Stir continuously and mix well to combine.
1. The mixture will thicken immediately.
1. After the mixture thickens turn off the heat.
1. While the mixture is hot, scoop out the batter using a spoon and roll them into small rounds.
1. Repeat the same with the remaining batter.
1. Steam the balls in the idli cooker or in pressure cooker for about 10 minutes till the dumplings turn shiny.
1. Transfer the Foxtail Millet Uppu Kozhukattai Recipes onto a serving plate and serve hot.
1. Serve Foxtail Millet Uppu Kozhukattai Recipes as a tea time snack with masala chai.
