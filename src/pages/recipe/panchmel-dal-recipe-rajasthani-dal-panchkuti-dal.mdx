### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Green Moong Dal (Whole).
- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- 1 Bay leaves (tej patta).
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies - broken.
- 1/4 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal).
- 1 Lemon - juice extracted.
- 1 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- Salt - as required.
- 1/4 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1/4 cup Black Urad Dal (Whole).
- 4 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/4 cup Masoor Dal (Whole).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 Green Chillies - slit vertically.
- 2 tablespoons Ghee.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini) - broken.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Rajasthani Panchmel Dal, we will first soak the all the dals in 3 cups of water for about 3 hours.
1.  Heat ghee in a pressure cooker over medium heat; add the cumin seeds and allow it to crackle.
1. Add the onion, green chillies, ginger and garlic and saute until the onion softends.
1. Add the bay leaf and cinnamon stick and saute for a few seconds until you can smell the aromas coming through.
1. Next add the tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, hing and saute for about a minute until the tomatoes have slightly softened.
1. Once the tomatoes have softened, add the soaked dals, salt to taste and add water if required such that the water is at least 2 inches above the level of dal.
1. Cover the pressure cooker, and pressure cook the Rajasthani Panchmel Dal for about 5 to 6 whistles - about 20 minutes until it is completely cooked through.
1. Turn off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Once the pressure has released, check if the dals are cooked well to perfection.
1. Once pressed between fingers it should mash easily.
1. Stir in the juice from the lemon and the coriander leaves and transfer the Rajasthani Panchmel Dal to a serving bowl.
1. The next is the tadka, heat ghee in a pan over medium heat.
1. Add the cumin seeds and red chillies and allow it to roast for a few seconds.
1. Turn off the heat and add this tadka to the Rajasthani Panchmel DalServe the Rajasthani Panchmel Dal along with Bati or eat it along Spicy Paneer Pulao With Vegetables followed by the mouth-watering Traditional Rasmalai Recipe.
