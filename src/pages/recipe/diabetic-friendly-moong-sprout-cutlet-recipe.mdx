### Ingredients

- Sunflower Oil - to shallow fry the tikkis.
- Salt - as per your taste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 cup Instant Oats (Oatmeal).
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 Green Chilli - roughly chopped.
- 2 cups Green Moong Sprouts - parboiled.
- 1 inch Ginger - roughly chopped.
- 1/2 cup Spring Onion Greens - finely chopped.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Diabetic Friendly Moong Sprout Cutlets, cook the sprouted green moong dal in a pressure cooker using 1/4 cup of water and a pinch of salt.
1. Cook for 2 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Save a hand full of the par boiled sprouted moong, and add the rest of the par boiled sprouts along with green chillies and ginger to a mixer and make a smooth mixture.
1. To this ground moong sprout mixture, add chopped spring onions, oats, coriander powder, garam masala, season with salt and mix well until all the ingredients come together.
1. Make small tikkis with the Moong Sprout mixture and keep them ready to pan fry them.
1. Heat a flat skillet on medium flame, brush a little oil on its surface, once its hot add a batch of moong sprout tikkis and let it cook on its bottom for a few minutes.
1. Once the moong sprout tikkis are golden brown on the bottom, flip over and cook the other side as well.
1. To cook one tikki, it will take up to 10 minutes for it to nicely brown on both sides.
1. Serve the Diabetic Friendly Moong Sprout Cutlet Recipe along with a Dip or Tomato Sauce and a cup of Masala Chai as an evening snack.
1. You can also make a chaat of this cutlet, by adding some onions, chutneys & sev and finally some chaat masala.
1. It will make a great party appetizer.
