### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Jaggery - optional.
- 1 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 4 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 tablespoons Mustard oil.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander (Dhania) Powder.
- 3 Potatoes (Aloo) - thickly sliced.
- 2 Onions - thinly sliced.
- 2 Karela (Bitter Gourd/ Pavakkai) - deseeded and cut into thin strips.

### Instructions

1. To begin with Bihari Karela Aloo Sabzi Recipe, prep all the ingredients and keep them aside.
1. Into the pressure cooker, add the karela, along with a little salt to taste and 2 tablespoons water and pressure cook for 2 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. Release the pressure immediately to retain the bright green color of the karela.
1. Keep this aside.
1. Heat oil in a kadai over medium heat; add the onion and garlic and saute until the onion is just soft.
1. Add the sliced potatoes, sprinkle salt and turmeric powder and cook the potatoes until they are well roasted and cooked.
1. You can optionally cover the pan and steam cook the potatoes so it cooks faster.
1. Once the potatoes are roasted, add the cooked karela and all the remaining spices like red chilli powder, garam masala powder, amchur powder, salt if needed and also jaggery if you like a little bit of sweetness.
1. Give it a good stir to combine all the ingredients.
1. Stir fry the Bihari Karela Aloo Sabzi for another 4 to 5 minutes so the flavours get well absorbed into the vegetable.
1. Once done, check the salt and spices and adjust to taste accordingly.
1. Turn off the heat and transfer the Bihari Karela Aloo Sabzi to a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. Serve Bihari Karela And Aloo Ki Sabzi along with Palak Dal, Phulkas and Jeera Rice for an everyday meal.
