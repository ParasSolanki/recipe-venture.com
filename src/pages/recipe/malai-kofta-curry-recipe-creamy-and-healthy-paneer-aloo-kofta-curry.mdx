### Ingredients

- Sunflower Oil - for cooking.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1-1/2 cups Paneer (Homemade Cottage Cheese) - crumbled.
- Salt - to taste.
- 3 Potatoes (Aloo) - boiled.
- 1/4 cup Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 1/4 cup Fresh cream.
- 1 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Honey.
- 1/2 cup Raisins - a handful.
- 4 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Gram flour (besan).
- 1 tablespoon Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves) - for garnishing.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 2 cups Homemade tomato puree.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee - for cooking.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Malai Kofta Recipe, combine all the ingredients for the koftas in a bowl and divide them into lemon size balls.
1. Flatten the balls in the palm of your hands and place a couple of raisins in the center.
1. Close the kofta dough back into a ball and keep aside.
1. The next step is to cook the Malai Koftas either in the Kuzhi Paniyaram PanIf you plan to use the Kuzhi Paniyaram Pan; then place the kofta balls in the pan's cavities; drizzle the koftas with a little oil and pan fry them until golden brown on both sides.
1. You will have to flip the kofta balls so they fry on all sides evenly.
1. The next step is to make the Kofta CurryHeat oil in a heavy bottomed pan; add in the grated ginger, garlic, onions and saut√© for a few seconds until the onions soften.
1. Stir in the tomato puree, honey, turmeric powder, cardamom powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, salt and chilli powder to taste.
1. Bring the kofta curry to a boil and simmer for a couple of minutes till the tomatoes get cooked through.
1. Finally add in the cream and the kasuri methi and stir it into the kofta curry.
1. Adjust the consistency of the malai kofta curry by adding water if required.
1. Do make sure to check the salt and spice levels and adjust according to your taste.
1. When you are ready to serve, place the kofta curry in the serving bowl and add the warm malai kofta balls in the curry, garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.
1. Serve Non Fried Malai Kofta Recipe with Tawa parathas and Mixed Vegetable Pulao Recipe and Mooli Raita for lunch or dinner.
1. You can even serve the Malai Kofta for a indian dinner party menu.
