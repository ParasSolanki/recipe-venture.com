### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Soy granules.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1/2 cup Rajma (Large Kidney Beans).
- 1 inch Ginger - grated.
- 2 cloves Garlic - grated.
- 1/4 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - small bunch.
- Salt - as per taste.
- Sunflower Oil - for shallow frying the cutlets.
- 1/4 cup Instant Oats (Oatmeal) - more to dust.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Red beans and Oat Cutlet Recipe, first clean, wash and soak red kidney beans in water overnight or for at least 7 - 8 hours.
1. Make powder of oats in grinder.
1. Keep aside until required.
1. In a pan boil 1 cup of water add soya granule bring to boil once.
1. Soya granules will puff up after one boil.
1. As soon as they swell up, turn off the heat and drain the soy granules using a colander and keep aside.
1. Once red kidney beans are soaked, in a pressure cooker add beans along with 1 cup of soaked water.
1. Add salt, give it a nice stir, cover the pressure cooker and place its weight on.
1. Cook the beans in the pressure cooker until you hear 4 to 5 whistles.
1. After 4 to 5 whistles, turn the heat to low and simmer the beans for another 15 minutes and then turn off the heat.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Once the pressure is released, open the cooker and drain beans in a colander.
1. Keep beans in the colander until all the excess water is drained off.
1. You can use this water to add it in your vegetable stock or even soups or rice.
1. To make the cutlets, in a large bowl add boiled beans, powdered oats reserving 2 tablespoon for dusting the cutlets, cooked soya granule, onion, salt, coriander leaves, red chili powder, cumin powder and salt to taste.
1. Mix nicely with dry fingers.
1.  Divide the red bean and oat cutlet mixture in equal portions.
1. Grease your palms shape each portion into a flat round of 1/2 inch thickness.
1. Place the shaped cutlets on a tray or plate.
1. Keep them in  the fridge for 10 minutes.
1. After 10 minutes bring out the cutlets from the fridge and dust with 2 tablespoon of powdered oats.
1. Next heat a heavy bottomed frying pan over medium heat to shallow fry the cutlets.
1. Place the oat coated cutlets on the heated pan, drizzle a teaspoon of oil over each of the red bean and oat cutlets.
1. Shallow fry each of them until golden brown on both sides and with a slotted spoon drain the cutlets on an absorbent paper.
1. Serve Red Beans and Oats Cutlet recipe as an evening snack along with Green Chutney and hot Ginger Cardamom Chai.
