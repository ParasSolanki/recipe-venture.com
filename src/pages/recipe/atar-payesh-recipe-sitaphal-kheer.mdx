### Ingredients

- Sugar - to taste.
- 100 ml Heavy whipping cream.
- 2 liter Milk.
- 1/4 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 4 Custard apple (Sitaphal).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Atar Payesh recipe, break each of the custard apples gently into halves, scoop out the flesh with the aid of a spoon.
1. Carefully remove the seeds, lightly mash the custard apple pulp with a fork.
1. Keep aside.
1. Set the milk to boil in a thick bottomed pan, add the green cardamom powder and let the milk simmer lazily over a low flame till it has reduced to almost half in volume.
1. Stir in the mashed custard apple pulp, continue to cook over a low flame for 5-7 odd minutes.
1. Add sugar to taste.
1. (The custard apples I used were very sweet and just a tablespoon of sugar was enough for me)Switch off the flame, fold in the whipped cream.
1. A hearty stir to ensure it is all mixed.
1. Serve chilled.
1. Serve Atar Payesh after a satisfying lunch of Cholar Dal, Bengali Luchi and Begun Bhaja on a weekday.
