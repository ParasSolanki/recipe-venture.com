### Ingredients

- 325 ml Lukewarm Water.
- 500 grams Vivatta Maida - or half of bread flour.
- 1 tablespoon Salt - or to taste.
- Egg white - for brushing (or milk).
- 30 grams Milk Powder.
- 14 grams Active dry yeast.
- 2 tablespoons Sugar.
- 3 tablespoons Butter (unsalted).

### Instructions

1. To begin making Pain Viennois | Vienna Bread Recipe, in a bowl, combine water, sugar, yeast and milk powder in a big mixing bowl.
1. Allow it to stand for 10 minutes until bubbly and frothy.
1. Add the flours, salt and butter to the yeast mixture and knead them to a dough.
1. Later transfer it over a work surface and continue kneading until smooth and pliable for about 10 minutes.
1. Do not use any extra flour while kneading or shaping.
1. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover the container with a plastic wrap.
1. Set aside in a warm place until almost tripled in volume.
1. Transfer the proofed dough on to a flat working surface and knead the dough for a minute or two to release the trapped air.
1. Divide the dough into 9 equal pieces and shape them into tight round balls.
1. Place them on a baking sheet and rest them for about 15 minutes.
1. Flatten each dough ball to remove the air bubbles and shape them into tight 8 inch logs.
1. Place 4 to 5 logs over each baking tray lined with parchment paper.
1. Brush their tops with milk or beaten egg.
1. Carefully score each log horizontally 12 to 15 times with a sharp blade or razor.
1. Allow them proof for about an hour until doubled.
1. Bake them in a preheated oven at 450 degree fahrenheit for about 15 minutes rotating the trays half way through to ensure equal baking.
1. Remove the baked buns and cool them on a wire rack to room temperature.
1. Vienna bread stays good for upto 2 days at room temperature and can be freezed for upto 3 weeks.
1. Serve Pain Viennois | Vienna Bread Recipe after grilling it with Braised Pork Belly Recipe or along with Beetroot Gazpacho Soup Recipe for a light dinner.
