### Ingredients

- Rose Petals - for garnishing.
- 4 tablespoons Condensed Milk.
- 4 tablespoons Milk.
- 1 teaspoon Poppy seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- Mixed nuts - for garnishing.
- Saffron strands - few.
- 1 tablespoon Cashew nuts - chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Badam (Almond) - blanched and peeled.
- 1 tablespoon Cashew nuts.
- 1 teaspoon Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 tablespoon Badam (Almond) - chopped.
- 2 liter Milk.
- 1 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 1-1/2 cup Thandai Powder.
- 1 tablespoon Pistachios - chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Pistachios.
- Sugar - as required.
- 2 tablespoons Basmati rice.

### Instructions

1. To begin with Thandai Flavored Rice Kheer, wash and soak the rice for 30 minutes.
1. After 30 minutes, drain the water and keep the rice aside.
1. Soak the saffron and thandai powder separately in lukewarm milk.
1. Powder the Thandai Powder ingredients - Almonds, Cashew nuts, Pistachios, poppy seeds, cardamom, cinnamon and whole black peppercorns into a coarse powder and keep aside.
1. The next step is to boil the milk.
1. When it comes to a boil, reduce the flame and add the rice in the saucepan.
1. Keep stirring the kheer until it reduces to one-third of the original quantity.
1. Once done, add sugar, milk-made, cardamom powder, chopped nuts, thandai milk and saffron.
1. Mix everything well and cook the Thandai Kheer for 5 minutes more.
1. After 5 minutes, turn off the flame and garnish it with chopped nuts and rose petals.
1. Serve Thandai Flavored Rice Kheer as a dessert after your delicious meal of Dal Makhani, Sev Tamatar Ki Sabzi, Phulka and Palak Raita.
