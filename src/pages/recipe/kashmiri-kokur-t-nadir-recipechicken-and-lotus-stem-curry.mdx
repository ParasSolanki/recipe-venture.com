### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Dry ginger powder.
- 1/4 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 1 inch Ginger - chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 3 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped for garnish.
- 2 teaspoon Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder.
- 1 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1/2 teaspoon Mint powder (Pudina).
- 2 cups Lotus Stem - (nadru or kamal kakdi) peeled & sliced.
- 2 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 3 tablespoon Mustard oil.
- 2 cups Water.
- 1-1/2 cups Curd (Dahi / Yogurt) - whisked.
- 750 grams Chicken - cut into medium pieces.
- 4 Cloves (Laung).
- 2 teaspoon Fennel Powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Kashmiri Kokur T Nadir recipe, wash and clean the chicken.
1. Mix the chicken with kashmiri red chilli powder, fennel powder and dry ginger powder in a bowl and keep it for marination for about 30 minutes.
1. Put the sliced lotus stem in warm water for few minutes to remove inside dirt and impurities.
1. Strain Lotus stem slices and pressure cook them along with 2 cups of water, bay leaf, fennel seeds, black cardamom, cloves, ginger slices and salt as per taste.
1. Cook till the cooker releases 2 to 3 whistles.
1. Leave it aside for pressure to settle down.
1. Heat mustard oil in a heavy bottomed pan.
1. Add cumin seeds and asafoetida powder into it, mix it well and cook it for 8 to 10 seconds.
1. Add the above marinated chicken along with the curd mix and cook in medium to low flames till yogurt starts to thicken and oil starts leaving from sides.
1. Now add the boiled and strained Lotus stem into the gravy and cook it on low flame for five minutes, stirring occasionally.
1. (you can add the spices as well) Now put caraway seeds into a Mortar and Pestle and crush them to form a powder.
1. Also crush dry Mint leaves between your hand and add into the gravy.
1. Adjust salt as needed and cook till the curry starts to thicken.
1. Once it is done, switch off the stove and garnish with some fresh coriander leaves.
1. Serve hot.
1. Serve Kashmiri Kokur T Nadir along with Kashmiri Dum Monji, Steamed Rice and Phulkas for a weekday meal with your family.
