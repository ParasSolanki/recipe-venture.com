### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 2 cups Methi Leaves (Fenugreek Leaves) - tightly packed.
- 2 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 cup Mangodi - moong dal.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Rajasthani Methi Mangodi Sabzi Recipe, rinse the fenugreek leaves in running water, drain and roughly chop them.
1. Keep it aside.
1. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a pan on medium heat and add the moong dal mangodi.
1. Fry the mangodi's until they turn slightly brown and keep aside.
1. Add the remaining oil in the same pan, add cumin seeds and red chillies.
1. Once the cumin seeds splutters add the finely chopped garlic and onion and saute until the onions are cooked.
1. Add the turmeric, red chilli powder and saute until the oil leaves the sides of the pan.
1. Add the fried mangodi back to the pan and mix well.
1. Now, combine 2 tablespoons of curd in half a cup of water until smooth and add it to the pan.
1. Bring the Rajasthani Methi Mangodi Sabzi to a rolling boil and add the chopped fenugreek leaves and saute until it is well cooked.
1. Season Rajasthani Methi Mangodi Sabzi with salt, garam masala, mix well and switch off the flame.
1. Transfer Rajasthani Methi Mangodi Sabzi into a serving dish and serve.
1. Serve the Rajasthani Methi Mangodi Sabzi Recipe, along with Rajasthani Kadhi and Phulkas for the weeknight dinner.
