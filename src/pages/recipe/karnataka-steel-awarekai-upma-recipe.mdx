### Ingredients

- 1/2 cup Dry coconut (kopra) - grated.
- 1 1/2 cups Avarekalu / Lilva Beans.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns - .
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Ginger - grated.
- 1/2 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1 Onion - chopped.
- 6 Curry leaves.
- 1/2  teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1/2 teaspoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1 1/4 cups Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 3 cups Water.
- 3 Green Chillies - slit.
- 2 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.

### Instructions

1. We begin making the Karnataka Style Avarekai Upma Recipe, heat a pan on medium flame, dry roast the sooji or rava till it is golden brown and keep it aside.
1. In a separate saucepan, add water and the avarekalu beans along with salt.
1. Bring to a single boil and turn off the flame.
1. Drain the water and keep it aside.
1. In a separate saucepan heat 3 cups water and keep it ready.
1. In a pan, heat oil on medium flame and add the mustard seeds, urad dal, channa dal, jeera seeds and then add the curry leaves, allowing the curry leaves to crackle.
1. Next add the chopped onions, saute till it is golden brown, next add the green chilies, ginger, turmeric powder and boiled avarekalu beans and give it a toss.
1. Add in the roasted rava and mix it well, once that is done slowly add in the boiling water.
1. Mix well, check for seasoning by adding salt and crushed pepper.
1. Let it boil and let the rava get cooked.
1. Once the rava is cooked mix it well and add the grated coconut and a tablespoon of ghee and toss well.
1. Serve the Karnataka Style Avarekai Upma Recipe along with Coconut Peanut Chutney Recipe and Jaggery Coffee Without Milk Recipe for breakfast.
