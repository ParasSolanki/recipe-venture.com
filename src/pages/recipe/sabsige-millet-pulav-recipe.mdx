### Ingredients

- 1 cup Kodo millet.
- 12 Cashew nuts - broken.
- 1-1/2 cups Dill leaves - finely chopped.
- 2 teaspoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1-1/2 teaspoons Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1/3 Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1/3 cup Green peas (Matar).
- 1 Potato (Aloo) - diced.
- 3 Green Chillies - adjustable.
- 1 Tomato - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Sabsige Millet Pulav recipe, in a mixer jar, add all ingredients for grinding and grind to a paste with as little water as possible.
1. In a pan heat some oil, splutter cumin seeds and fry the cashews, then add the tomatoes, cook for 30 seconds to a minute now add potatoes and peas.
1. Add the finely chopped dill leaves and saute until the greens wilt and turn fragrant for approximately 2-3 minutes.
1. Now add the paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
1. Add washed millets and mix until well combined.
1. Toss around for a minute.
1. Add 1-3/4 cup of water, salt to taste.
1. Bring this to a boil and turn the heat to simmer and cook with the lid cracked open slightly for 12-15 minutes on low heat or until all the liquid has evaporated.
1. Turn off the heat and gently transfer to a serving bowl immediately or the residual heat can make the millets mushy.
1. Serve Sabsige Millet Pulav along with Boondi Raita, Palak Raita or any other raita of your choice.
