### Ingredients

- Salt - as required.
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- Water - as required.
- 1-1/2 cups All Purpose Flour (Maida).
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - Handful.
- 3 tablespoons Ghee.
- 1/2 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Lemon juice - (adjust).
- 3 tablespoon Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).
- 2 cups Green peas (Matar) - blanched and water drained off completely.
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt.
- 1/2 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- Mint Leaves (Pudina) - Handful.
- 2 Green Chillies - chopped or as required.
- 2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 2 teaspoons Cumin seeds (Jeera) - roasted.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Matar Karanji first make the dough for the covering.
1. For the karanji/gujiya dough:Mix the flour, semolina, salt and ghee.
1. Rub together the mixture for 1-2 minutes till the ghee is completely incorporated.
1. You can use a food processor if you have one.
1. Gradually add water just enough to form a stiff but pliable dough (about 4-5 tablespoons water should be sufficient)Keep the dough covered for about 30 minutes.
1. For the Gujiya/Karanji filling:In a mixer grinder, add the coconut, green chillies, coriander, mint leaves and cumin seeds and grind to a smooth paste.
1. Do not add water.
1. In a mixing bowl add the peas, ground coconut mixture, ajwain, salt and lemon juice.
1. Mix until all ingredients are combinedTo make the Matar Karanji:Heat oil for deep frying in a wok.
1. Pinch off small, lime sized balls of the dough and roll out into a thin circle about 3-4 inch in diameter.
1. Use some oil or ghee to grease the working surface (do not use flour)Place about 2-3 teaspoons of the peas filling on one side of the circle leaving some space on the edge.
1. Fold over the other half of the circle to make a half moon shape.
1. Press and seal the edge taking care that air pockets do not form inside.
1. Use a Karanji cutter or a small fluted pastry cutter/wheel to remove excess dough and shape the Karanji.
1. Deep fry on medium heat till golden brown.
1. (You can shape the karanjis at one time.
