### Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon Melon seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 tablespoons Ghee - or oil.
- 5 Badam (Almond).
- 3/4 cup Coconut milk.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 tablespoon Cashew nuts.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1/2 tablespoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Raisins.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2-3 tablespoons Anardana Powder (Pomegranate Seed Powder) - Seeds.
- 5 Cashew nuts.
- 1-1/2 cup Mixed vegetables - (potato.
- 1 Tomato - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin with Navratan Korma, soak cashew, almond and melon seeds in water for 30 minutes to prepare a paste.
1. Drain the water completely.
1. Peel off the almond skin and blend all in a mixer to smooth paste adding a little water.
1. Keep it aside.
1. Heat 2 tablespoons ghee or oil in a pan or a kadhai.
1. Add chopped onions and saute a while.
1. Next, add ginger-garlic paste and stir well.
1. Add tomato and give a stir.
1. Add turmeric powder, chilli powder and coriander powder and mix well everything.
1. Add ground paste and 1/4 cup water.
1. Cook it uncovered for 2 mins and add mixed vegetables and 1 cup water.
1. Cook it covered on low-medium flame for 10-15 minutes.
1. Add coconut milk, garam masala and salt to taste.
1. Mix well everything and cook again for another 10 minutes.
1. Meanwhile, roast cashew and raisin in ghee and add this to the korma.
1. Garnish with pomegranate seeds and your dish is ready to be served.
1. Serve Navratan Korma with Whole Wheat Lachha Parathas and Mushroom Pulav for a delicious lunch or dinner.
