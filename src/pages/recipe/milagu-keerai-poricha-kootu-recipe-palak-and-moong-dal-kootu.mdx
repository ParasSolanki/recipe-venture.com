### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves - roughly torn.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin (Jeera) seeds.
- 1/2 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 300 grams Red Amaranth Leaves - washed and finely chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 teaspoon Coconut Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 cup Yellow Moong Dal (Split).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Milagu Keerai Poricha Kootu Recipe, into a pressure cooker, add the amaranth, a pinch of salt along with 2 tablespoons of water.
1. Cover the cooker and pressure cook for about a whistle and turn off the heat.
1. Place the cooker under running water to release the pressure immediately.
1. This process is important to not over cook the amaranth and keep its colour.
1. Open the lid of the cooker and keep aside.
1. The next step is to make the dal.
1. Add the moong dal to the pressure cooker along with, turmeric powder, salt and 2 cups of water, .
1. Cover the cooker and pressure cook the moong dal for 4 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Once the pressure gets released, open the cooker and give a stir or a light whisk.
1. Now, we will proceed to make the Keerai Poricha Kootu recipe.
1. Into the mixer jar, add the cumin seeds and black pepper corn and the freshly grated coconut.
1. Add 1/4 cup of water and blend to make a coarse paste.
1. Stir in the cooked keerai/amaranth into the cooked dal.
1. Add the ground kootu masala into the moong dal and keerai mixture and stir well.
1. Check the salt and adjust taste accordingly and bring to a brisk boil.
1. The final step is to make the tadka or the seasoning.
1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat.
1. Add the mustard seeds, cumin seeds and urad dal.
1. Allow them to crackle and the dal to turn golden brown.
1. Finally add the curry leaves and turn off the heat.
1. Add the seasoning mixture to the Keerai Kootu and stir well.
1. Place the Keerai Kootu, which is still in the pressure cooker and give it a brisk boil.
1. You can optionally add a tablespoon of ghee or butter to enhance the taste.
1. Once done, transfer the Milagu Keerai Poricha Kootu to a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. Serve Milagu Keerai Poricha Kootu for your South Indian lunch along with steamed rice or Brown Rice, Thakkali Rasam Recipe  and Urulaikizhangu Roast.
1. You can also make this for an Indian dinner and serve it along with Phulkas and Boondi Raita.
