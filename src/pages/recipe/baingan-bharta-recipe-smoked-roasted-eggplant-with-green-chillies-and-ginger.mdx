### Ingredients

- 4 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped for garnish.
- 1 Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant).
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 Green Chillies - finely chopped.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 2 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Ghee.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Baingan Bharta Recipe , first prep all the ingredients and keep them ready.
1. The brinjal needs to be roasted and can be roasted in two ways; in the oven or on the gas flame.
1. Method using the oven for roastingPreheat the oven to 180 C.
1. Place the brinjal on a baking sheet and place in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes or until the brinjal has started to soften and the outer skin is charred.
1. When you prick inside with a knife, you can feel that the brinjal has become tender, if not continue to roast until done.
1. Method using the flame for roastingPlace the brinjal on the stove top flame, with continuous monitoring, using tongs to turn the brinjal around while it is roasting.
1. After about 10 minutes you will notice that the brinjal’s skin begins to char and the inside begins to get tender.
1. To test, when you insert a knife you will feel it is soft and not hard.
1. If hard continue to roast until done.
1. Once brinjal is roasted allow the brinjal to cool down.
1. Peel the charred skin of the cooled brinjal and discard the skin.
1. Coarsely mash the pulp using a fork or mince finely using a knife.
1. Heat ghee in a pan over medium high heat.
1. Add the cumin seeds and allow seeds to crackle.
1. Add the grated ginger and chopped onions, sauté over medium heat until the onions are tender.
1. Then add the chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, green chillies, coriander powder, garam masala powder and chilli powder.
1. Stir to combine and sauté until all the ingredients come together for about couple of minutes and the tomatoes become soft and mushy.
1. Stir in the roasted brinjal and salt.
1. Turn the heat to low and simmer the Baingan Bharta for about 10 minutes or so until it gets well combined with the tomatoes and spices.
1. Once done turn off the heat, check the salt and seasonings and adjust to taste accordingly.
1. Finally garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves and serve hot.
1. Serve the Baingan Bharta Recipe along with hot Kadai Paneer, Dal Makhani, Phulka to enjoy your special weekend meal with family.
