### Ingredients

- 500 grams Baby Potatoes.
- 4 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 tablespoons Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 tablespoons Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - for garnish.
- Salt - to taste.
- 3 Bay leaves (tej patta).
- 4 Tomatoes - pureed.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 6 Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 2 tablespoons Dry coconut (kopra) - grated.
- 3/4 cup Milk.
- 2 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 Onion - grated.
- 2 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 tablespoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 4 Cloves (Laung).
- 1 tablespoon Poppy seeds.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Lahori Aloo recipe, firstly wash, boil and peel the baby potatoes.
1. Keep aside for cooling.
1. Once cool, heat the oil in a non-stick pan, add the potatoes and salt, mix well and sauté on a medium flame for 3 to 4 minutes till slightly browned.
1. Keep them aside.
1. Heat the oil in a kadhai or heavy bottom pan, add the onions, bayleaves and sauté on a medium flame for 2-3 minutes till onions turn translucent.
1. Add the ginger-garlic paste, mix well and cook on a medium flame for a minute.
1. Add the spice paste and mix well.
1. Sauté further on a medium flame for 1 minute.
1. Add salt, tomato pulp, mix well and cook on a medium flame for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring continuously so that it doesn’t stick to bottom.
1. Add the browned potatoes and milk, and let the curry simmer for 5 to 6 minutes, stirring occasionally.
1. Garnish the Lahori Aloo dish with coriander.
1. Serve Lahori Aloo with Whole Wheat Lachha Paratha and Lauki Raita for a perfect weekday meal.
