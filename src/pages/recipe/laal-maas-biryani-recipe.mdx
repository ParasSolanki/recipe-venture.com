### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Mint Leaves (Pudina) - finely chopped.
- 2 Kashmiri dry red chillies.
- 2 Green Chillies - slit.
- 10 Kashmiri dry red chillies - soaked in water for at least 20 minutes.
- 2 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 1/2 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 3 Onions - thinly sliced.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 cups Basmati rice - washed and soaked for 30 minutes.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1/4 cup Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 3 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 2 Onions - thinly sliced.
- 1/4 cup Ghee.
- 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- Charcoal - pieces for smoking.
- 2 tablespoons Garlic - paste.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 1 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 3 Cloves (Laung).
- 500 grams Mutton - on the bone.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 tablespoons Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder.
- 2 tablespoons Ginger - paste.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander (Dhania) Powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 tablespoons Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 2 teaspoons Cumin powder (Jeera).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Laal Maas Biryani Recipe, we will first make the laal maas.
1. To make the Laal Maas - We will first make the Laal maas masala First, soak the Kashmiri red chillies in water for about 10 minutes.
1.  Once done, heat a saucepan with water, halfway through the pan, on medium flame and bring it to a rolling boil.
1. Once it comes to a rolling boil, reduce the flame, and add all the whole spices - kashmiri red dry chillies, cumin seeds, black cardamom, green cardamom, cinnamon stick, cloves and bay leaf.
1. Let it boil for about 5-7 minutes and then turn off the flame.
1. Drain the excess water and set aside.
1. Do not discard the water.
1.  Cool the boiled spices to room temperature and grind to a smooth paste using very little water.
1. Transfer the Laal Maas Masala to a bowl and set aside.
1. To marinate the mutton for the laal maas In a mixing bowl, combine the washed and cleaned mutton along with yogurt, freshly ground laal maas masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, salt and ginger garlic paste.
1. Nicely massage the marinade on to the mutton pieces, cover and set aside for a minimum of 30 minutes.
1. To make the Laal Maas Masala CurryIn a kadai heat ghee on medium flame, add the sliced onions and saute until they turn into a lovely brown colour.
1. This will take about 10 minutes on low medium heat stirring continuously, to get an even colour.
1. To the browned onions, now add the ginger garlic paste, and continue to cook for yet another minute.
1. At this stage, add the marinated mutton pieces along with the marinade and give it a good stir.
1. Add salt to taste, and about 1/2 cup of water, and allow the mutton to cook on medium-low heat for about 25 minutes or until the mutton is 85% cooked.
1. Turn on the flame and light a piece of charcoal until burning red hot.
1. Once its well lit, transfer the charcoal piece, into a small katori, and add 2 cloves into this same katori.
1. Now gently place the charcoal katori in the centre of laal maas kadai, pour about 2 teaspoons of ghee over  it and immediately close the kadai with a lid.
1. Allow the smoke to be absorbed by the laal maas.
1. Open the lid after 3-4 minutes, remove the katori with the coal; add the garam masala and mix it properly.
1. Set this laal maas masala curry aside.
1. You can eat this laal maas curry along with rice or even phulka for lunch or dinner.
1. To make the Laal maas biryani Heat a pressure cooker with ghee on medium flame, to this add the whole spices - bay leaf, kashmiri red chilli, big cardamom, small cardamom, cloves and cinnamon.
1. Once these whole spices begin to sizzle, add the thinly sliced onions and saute until they turn into a light brown colour.
1. Once the onions have browned, add the ginger garlic paste, green chillies and salt.
1. Continue to cook for about a minute or two.
1. Then add the coriander powder, turmeric powder and kashmiri red chilli powder.
1. Stir well, now add the pre cooked laal maas, meat and gravy included and give it a good mix until well combined.
1. Finally, add the washed and drained rice, 4 cups of water, coriander and mint leaves and give it a quick stir.
1. Adjust the salt to suit your taste.
1. Close the pressure cooker and pressure cook the Laal Maas Biryani for 2-3 whistles and turn off the flame.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally and then open the pressure cooker.
1.  Give the Laal Maas Biryani a gentle stir taking care to keep the Biryani rice separate.
1. Transfer the Laal Maas Biryani to a platter and serve piping hot.
1. Serve Laal Maas Biryani Recipe along with some Lachedar Kakdi Pyaz Kachumber Recipe and Onion Raita Recipe - Pyaz Ka Raita Recipe for a complete meal.
