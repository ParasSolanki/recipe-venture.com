### Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Roasted Peanuts (Moongphali).
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/4 cup Tamarind Paste - thick.
- 3 tablespoons Fresh coconut - grated.
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 tablespoons Sambar Powder.
- 3 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 2 Green Chillies - slit (optional).
- 3 tablespoons Jaggery - (reserve 1 tablespoon).
- 10 Curry leaves.
- 3 cups Poha (Flattened rice).
- Asafoetida (hing) - a pinch.
- 2 teaspoons Mustard seeds.
- 2 tablespoons Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - finely diced (optional).
- 2 teaspoons White Urad Dal (Split).
- 3 tablespoons Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 2 Onions - finely diced.

### Instructions

1. To begin with the Gojju Avalakki Recipe, firstly combine tamarind extract, jaggery, salt, sambar powder in a mixing bowl and mix everything properly.
1. To this, add the Avalakki and mix to coat everything well, now sprinkle some water (too much water can make it mushy) on top of the Avalakki and let it soak for 20-30 minutes and absorb the tamarind liquid.
1. After 20 to 30 minutes, heat required oil in a wide pan.
1. Add mustard seeds and let it splutter.
1. Add the urad dal, chana dal, groundnuts and cook on low flame for 2 minutes stirring in between so that they get an even colour.
1. To this add the asafoetida, green chillies, curry leaves and give it a stir.
1. Add in the chopped onions, turmeric powder and some salt.
1. This quickens the cooking process of onions, once they start to shrink, add the capsicum and give it a nice mix.
1. This whole step should take about 5-6 minute on a medium flame.
1. Add the dry tamarind powder and the reserved jaggery.
1. This enhances the taste of the onions when combined with the poha.
1. (The Avalakki is like a sponge it soaks in everything and turns bland very soon) This steps helps its stay flavourful longer.
1. Now add the Avalakki into the tadka and gently mix it all around.
1. Adjust salt according to your taste.
1. Garnish it with coriander leaves and grated coconut.
1. Serve Gojju Avalakki Recipe along with hot Masala Chai and Peanut Butter Banana Sandwich for a delicious weekday breakfast.
