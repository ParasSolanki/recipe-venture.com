### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- 1/4 cups Roasted Peanuts (Moongphali).
- Salt - to taste.
- 20 Curry leaves.
- 1/2 cup Red khara sev.
- 2 cups Poha (Flattened rice).
- 1/4 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal) - roasted.
- 1/2 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder - adjut.
- 1/2 cup Boondi.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Aval mixture recipe, first heat oil in a small wok/kadhai.
1. Place the Poha in a medium sized sieve with a handle and dip it in the hot oil.
1. Once the Poha sizzles take them out of the oil, drain it and place on an absorbent paper.
1. Do this in batches, 1/2 cup at a time.
1. Fry the groundnuts, roasted chana dal, curry leaves separately in the same oil and drain on absorbent paper.
1. Take the oil off the heat.
1. Keep 1 tablespoon oil in the wok and take out the rest.
1. Add the hing, turmeric powder and red chilli powder to the hot oil in the wok.
1. Pour this over the Poha.
1. In a large mixing bowl add the poha, groundnuts, roasted chana dal, curry leaves, sev, boondi and toss it together to mix.
1. Add salt if required.
1. Cool and store it in an airtight container.
1. Serve Aval Mixture with Kesari Gujiya and Masala Chai to your guests during festivals and special occasions.
