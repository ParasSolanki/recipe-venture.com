### Ingredients

- 2 cups Coconut milk - thin.
- 2 Green Chillies.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Sunflower Oil - as required.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- Curry leaves - a few.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 Mustard seeds.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 2 Bay leaves (tej patta).
- 1 cup Coconut milk - thick.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - for garnishing.
- 200 grams Boondi - kara boondi.
- 1 Tomato - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Kara Boondi Kurma Recipe, heat oil in a heavy bottomed pan on medium flame.
1. Add mustard seeds and allow it to splutter.
1. After 10 seconds, add bay leaves, cinnamon stick and cloves.
1. Saute for about 30 seconds.
1. Next, add onions and cook till they turn soft and translucent.
1. Once the onions turn soft, add ginger garlic paste, slit green chillies, curry leaves, salt and turmeric powder.
1. Mix everything well.
1. Add tomatoes, red chilli powder, coriander powder and let it cook till the tomatoes turn mushy.
1. This will take about 3 to 4 minutes.
1. Add thin coconut milk and let it boil.
1. Once it comes to boil, add the boondi.
1. Once the gravy becomes thick, switch off the heat, add thick coconut milk and give it a stir.
1. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.
1. Serve Kara Boondi Kurma along with Tawa Paratha and Palak Raita for an everyday meal.
1. You can also serve it with Crisp Ghee Roast Dosa for your lunch.
