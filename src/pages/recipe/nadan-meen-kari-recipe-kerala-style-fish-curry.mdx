### Ingredients

- 2 Tomatoes - pureed.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 cup Shallots - chopped.
- Water - as required.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel Powder.
- 4 tablespoon Coconut Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 tablespoon Red Chilli powder - (add more if you like more spicy).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 4 Kokum (Malabar Tamarind).
- 500 grams Sardine fish - (mathi.
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Black pepper powder.
- 1/2 Turmeric powder (Haldi).

### Instructions

1. To prepare Nadan Meen Curry or Kerala Style Fish Curry, Clean the fish well and cut into pieces.
1. Add salt and turmeric powder to the fish and let it sit for 15 minutes.
1. Then wash the fish again well.
1. Add 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 teaspoon red chili powder and 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder.
1. Rub it all over the fish, place them inside the fridge and leave it to marinade for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
1. Heat coconut oil in a clay pot.
1. When oil turns hot add mustard seeds and wait till it crackles.
1. Then add chopped shallots and saut√© till onions turn pink.
1. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook till raw smell of the paste is gone.
1. Add tomato puree and cook until the puree turns thick and oil separates from the sides of the pan.
1. Mix all the spice powder with 1 tablespoon water into a thick paste.
1. Add this paste into the pan and cook for about 3 minutes.
1. Add 1 cup water and bring it to boil.
1. Add salt to taste and continue to boil for another 3 minutes.
1. Now add kodumpuli and boil for another 10 to 15 minutes until the curry turns a little thick.
1. Now add the marinated fish, reduce flame, cover and continue to cook on low flame for about 7 to 10 minutes or until the fish is cooked well.
1. Turn off flame.
1. Nadan meen kari is ready to be served with steamed rice.
