### Ingredients

- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1/2 cup Black Urad Dal (Whole).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 1 cup Idli Rice.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.

### Instructions

1. To make the Kanchipuram Idlis, first soak the rice, urad dal and fenugreek seeds in water for about 5 hours, or overnight.
1. Rinse the soaked grains once or twice and grind into a slightly thick coarse batter with very little water.
1. Add salt, mix well and place it in a steel vessel with enough space for it to rise as it ferments.
1. Let it rest overnight.
1. Place a pan on the heat and warm some oil/ghee.
1. Add cumin seeds, pepper corns, curry leaves, finely chopped ginger, and saut√© for a minute.
1. Add asafetida and mix again.
1. Turn off heatAdd this prepared tempering to the fermented batter and mix well.
1. Grease idli plates with oil, pour the batter into them and place them in a steamer.
1. Add enough water into the steamer, shut the lid and allow it to steam it for 10 to 12 minutes until the Idli is cooked.
1. If a spoon inserted inside the idli should come out clean, and the idli should be soft and fluffy.
1. Remove the idli from steamer and cool it for 3 minutes.
1. With the help of butter knife scrape out the idli from the moulds and serve hot with Sambar, Kathirikai Gothsu, Chutney Podi or Coconut Chutney.
