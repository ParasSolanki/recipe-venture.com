### Ingredients

- 1/2 cup Jaggery.
- 6 tablespoons Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1/4 cup Tamarind Paste - (Or Soaked tamarind pulp - 1/2 cup).
- 12 Dry Red Chillies - (keep aside half of them for seasoning).
- 1/4 cup White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1-1/2 cups Sunflower Oil.
- 20 Curry leaves.
- 3 tablespoons Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 cup Dry coconut (kopra) - grated.
- 1/2 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/2 cup Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 1/2 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1/2 cup Roasted Peanuts (Moongphali).

### Instructions

1. To begin making Chettinad Style Puliyogare Gojju Recipe, get prep with all the ingredients mentioned and keep them handy.
1. In a kadai, dry roast each of the ingredients individually one at a time, on a low heat - Urad Dal, Chana Dal - both until slightly brown, black pepper, Cumin seeds, Red Chillies a bit until fragrant.
1. Allow to cool by spreading them on a large plate.
1. Once cooled, grind all the above dry roasted ingredients by using a mixer grinder.
1. Set aside.
1. Next dry roast these ingredients: Sesame seeds, Dry coconut.
1. Coarsely grind both of these ingredients and mix it with the ground spices you prepared in mixer earlier.
1. In an aluminium vessel, or a thick bottomed Kadai, heat about 2 cups of water, add jaggery, tamarind pulp, salt to taste and bring to a boil.
1. Check on the tangy v/s sweet admixture of flavours.
1. Adjust as per requirement by adding in more jaggery/tamarind pulp - as this is the mixture that gives the rice its unique flavour.
1. Allow to cool.
1. Once the jaggery - tamarind mixture cools down add the ground dal spice & coconut powder and stir well to form a nice thick semi dry dough like consistency.
1. Set aside.
1. In a kadai/wok, heat oil, add in the mustard seeds to splutter, curry leaves & dry red chillies.
1. Add the groundnuts and fry for about 2-3 minutes on a low flame.
1. To the above mixture, finally, add asafoetida/ hing powder.
1. While it's still hot transfer it into the kadai in which is the jaggery tamarind and spice powder mixture is resting.
1. Stir well so that the oil and tempered spices coat the overall mixture and help in preserving it for a longer time.
1. Chettinad Style Puliyogare Gojju Recipe is ready, you can store this in an air tight container in a refrigerator for weeks together.
1. Just take a few table spoons of the gojju and mix it with cooked plain rice, as and when you want to relish this tangy goodness.
1. Serve Chettinad Style Puliyogare Gojju Recipe with Aama Vadai, or Akki Peni Sandige for breakfast or lunch.
