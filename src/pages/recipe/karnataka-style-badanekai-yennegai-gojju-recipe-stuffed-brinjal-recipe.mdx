### Ingredients

- 1 Onion - sliced.
- 4 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 teaspoon Kala jeera.
- 2 tablespoon Fresh coconut - grated.
- 2 teaspoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 1 tablespoon Raw Peanuts (Moongphali).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 cup Pearl onions (Sambar Onions).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- Salt - Salt to taste.
- 2 teaspoon Sesame seeds (Til seeds).
- 18 grams Tamarind.
- 3 cloves Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves.
- Sunflower Oil - Oil for cooking.
- 3 Cloves (Laung).
- 6 Small Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - quatered.
- 1 teaspoon Jaggery - powdered.
- 1 tablespoon Poppy seeds.

### Instructions

1. We begin making the Karnataka Style Badanekai Yennegai Gojju Recipe (Stuffed Brinjal Recipe) by dry roasting the ingredients like jeera seeds, coriander seeds, peanuts, dry red chillies, poppy seeds, cloves and cinnamon stick.
1. Add all these roasted ingredients into a grinder along with coconut, pearl onions, tamarind and jaggery into a nice smooth paste.
1. You can add little water while grinding but not a lot.
1. Now Stuff the ground masala mixture into the brinjal and keep it aside.
1. Keep the leftover masala also to add it into the gravy.
1. Heat a pressure cooker with oil on a medium heat, crackle some mustard seeds and sliced onions and fry them till the onions become translucent.
1. Then add these stuffed brinjals and fry them well till it becomes soft.
1. Move them carefully without breaking them.
1. You can cover and cook it as well.
1. Once the brinjal is half cooked, add the remaining masala with 1/2 cup water and cook on a medium heat.
1. Cook for about 2 whistle.
1. Release the pressure naturally and then keep saut√©ing till the masala thickens.
1. Some of the oil oozes out from the side.
1. Once done sprinkle with some chopped coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve the Karnataka Style Badanekai Yennegai Gojju Recipe (Stuffed Brinjal Recipe) along with Jolada Roti Recipe (Jowar Bhakri) and Nuggekai Kharbyaali Recipe (North Karnataka Spiced Drumstick Sambar) to go along with some steamed rice.
