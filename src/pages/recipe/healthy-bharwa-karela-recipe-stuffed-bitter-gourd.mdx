### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder.
- 1/2 inch Fresh turmeric root - or 1 teaspoon turmeric powder.
- 1 Green Chilli.
- 4 cloves Garlic.
- 1 Tomato.
- 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 8 Karela (Bitter Gourd/ Pavakkai).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1/4 cup Mango (Raw) - chopped.
- 1 Onion - (red or white).
- 1 inch Ginger - peeled.
- Tamarind - marble sized.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- Sunflower Oil - coconut oil or mustard oil as required (maximum 3 tablespoons).
- 10 Raw Peanuts (Moongphali).

### Instructions

1. To begin making Healthy Bharwa Karela Recipe (Indian Masala Stuffed Bitter Gourd), scrape the outer skin of the bitter gourds and make a deep vertical cut in the center leaving the base portion intact.
1. Gently scoop out the seeds and the pith and set it aside.
1. Rub the bitter gourds with salt, inside out.
1. Mix about 1/2 teaspoon of salt to the pith and seeds and allow it to rest for at least half an hour.
1. Squeeze excess water and wash and drain them.
1. Pat the bitter gourds with a kitchen towel.
1. In the meantime, grind all the ingredients given under 'Ground Masala' in a mixer grinder including onion, tomato, raw mango, green chilli, garlic, ginger and fresh turmeric root.
1. Heat a pan and gently roast the ingredients given under the 'dry masala' except coconut including raw peanuts, coriander seeds, dry red chillies and tamarind.
1. Once it is done, grind them coarsely with coconut.
1. Keep it aside.
1. Heat a pan/ kadai over a low flame with a little oil.
1. When the oil heats up add the ground tomato onion masala and the pith of karela.
1. Mix dry (roasted) masala, kashmiri red chili powder, amchur powder and garam masala.
1. Add salt as required.
1. Adjust spices.
1. Allow it to cook for 8-10 minutes or till the time the excess water is absorbed and looks cooked, switch off.
1. Spoon the masala into each processed bitter gourd/Karela.
1. Heat a wide pan with 1-2 tablespoons of oil.
1. Gently place the stuffed bitter gourds in the pan and shallow fry them till all the sides are uniformly browned.
1. You can keep the lid closed often to have them cooked evenly.
1. Delicious Healthy Bharwa Karela (Indian Masala Stuffed Bitter Gourd) is now all set to be served.
1. Serve Bharwa Karela along with Palak Dal, Phulka and Boondi Raita for an everyday meal.
