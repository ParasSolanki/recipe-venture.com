### Ingredients

- 200 grams Paneer (Homemade Cottage Cheese) - cubed.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 Onion - finely sliced.
- Curry leaves - a few.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- Whole Black Peppercorns - a few.
- 1/3 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 Tomatoes - cut in wedges.
- 1 Kokum (Malabar Tamarind) - soaked in 2 tablespoon water.
- 200 ml Coconut milk.
- 1/4 cup Water.
- 2 teaspoon Coconut Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - a few sprigs for garnishing.
- 1 teaspoon Black pepper powder.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder - (dajust to taste).

### Instructions

1. To begin making Fusion Style Paneer Malai Makhani Recipe, prep all the ingredients needed for this recipe.
1. In a kadai heat coconut oil, when it is hot enough, add in the cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds and peppercorns and let them sizzle.
1. Wait for about 10 seconds or till sizzling, and add in the curry leaves.
1. Wait till they splutter.
1. (takes about a couple of seconds)Add the onion and sauté for about 3-5 minutes till the onion is tender.
1. Now add in the ginger, garlic paste, some salt and turmeric powder.
1. Sauté till the raw smell goes and appears even.
1. Now add in the coriander powder, black pepper powder and red chilli powder, continue frying for another 2 minutes or till the spices are evenly mixed.
1. Add the tomatoes and fry for another 2 minutes or so, till the tomatoes are softened.
1. Cover the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes till tomatoes are fully cooked.
1. Add the paneer(cottage cheese) cubes and mix it well but careful not to break the cubes.
1. Add the kokum or kokum extract, coconut milk and water.
1. Adjust salt.
1. Cover the pan and simmer for about 3-5 minutes on a low medium heat.
1. Switch off heat and garnish Paneer Malai Makhani with fresh coriander.
1. Serve Paneer Malai Makhani Recipe with Pudina Tawa Paratha or phulkas with Indian Style Black Chickpea Salad Recipe for a complete meal.
