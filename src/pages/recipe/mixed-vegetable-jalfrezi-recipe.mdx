### Ingredients

- Salt - as required.
- 1 cup Green peas (Matar).
- 1 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 tablespoon Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves).
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 cup Green beans (French Beans) - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 Tomatoes.
- 1 Onion.
- 1/2 teaspoon Sugar.
- 1/2 cup Sweet corn.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - for garnishing.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 cup Carrot (Gajjar) - diced vetically.
- 2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Mixed Vegetable Jalfrezi Recipe, wash all the vegetables and cut into thin strips.
1. Chop onion lengthwise and prepare the tomato puree.
1. In a kadai, add some oil.
1. Once the oil is warm, add the ginger garlic paste and saute for a minute.
1. The paste will splutter when added to oil, so place the stove on low-medium heat.
1. Next, add chopped onions and saute until translucent.
1. Once done add in the tomato puree and cook until the tomatoes turn mushy.
1. After the tomatoes have turned soft add the turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder and garam masala powder to it.
1. Saute until oil separates.
1. Now add the chopped veggies and fresh green peas to it and saute for a minute.
1. After a minute add salt and required water for cooking vegetables.
1. Stir and cook covered until vegetables gets cooked well but not mushy.
1. It has to retain its crunchiness.
1. Switch off the flame.
1. Add sugar and crushed kasuri methi to Mixed Vegetable Jalfrezi, and mix well.
1. Transfer Mixed Vegetable Jalfrezi  into a serving bowl and garnish with coriander leaves.
1. Serve Mixed Vegetable Jalfrezi along with Phulkas and Palak Raita for a weeknight dinner.
