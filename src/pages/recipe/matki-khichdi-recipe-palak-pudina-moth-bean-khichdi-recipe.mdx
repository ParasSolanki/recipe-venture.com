### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 cup Spinach - roughly chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 4 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1 cup Moth Dal (Matki) - soaked overnight.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 3/4 cup Rice.
- 2 teaspoons Ghee.
- 1/2 cup Mint Leaves (Pudina) - roughly chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Black pepper powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Hariyali Matki Khichdi (Palak Pudina Moth Bean Khichdi Recipe), wash and soak the moth beans overnight.
1. Wash and soak the rice for 10 minutes before starting to cook.
1. Heat 2 teaspoons of ghee in a pressure pan, add the cumin seeds, whole peppercorns and let it crackle.
1. Add the asafoetida, onions and saute until it turns translucent.
1. Once the onions turn translucent, add the chopped spinach and mint leaves, saute until it wilts a bit.
1. Drain and add the soaked moth beans and rice.
1. Add 4 cups of water, season with salt, garam masala and pressure cook the Matki Khichdi for 3 to 4 whistles.
1. Switch off the heat.
1. And let the pressure release naturally.
1. Open the lid and gently mix the Matki Khichdi together.
1. Now, heat the remaining ghee in a small tadka pan, add the chopped garlic cloves and fry until it turns golden.
1. Switch off the heat.
1. Pour this crispy garlic tadka over the prepared Matki Khichdi.
1. Garnish with more fresh mint leaves and serve Hariyali Matki Khichdi recipe along with Rajasthani Kadhi, Satvik Sprouts Salad and Roasted Papad for a weeknight dinner.
