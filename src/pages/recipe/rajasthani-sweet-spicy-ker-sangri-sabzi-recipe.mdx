### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 Dry Red Chillies - broken into pieces.
- 1-1/2 teaspoons Red Chilli powder.
- Sunflower Oil - for cooking.
- 1/4 cup Raisins.
- 3/4 cup Sangri.
- 1 tablespoon Jaggery - optional.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 4 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves.
- 2 tablespoons Ker.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Ker Sangri Sabzi Recipe, soak the ker and sangri immersed in lot of water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
1. After 8 hours, drain the water and rinse the ker sangri in water a couple of more times, to remove residual dust and dirt.
1. In the next step, we will cook the soaked ker sangri in the pressure cooker.
1. Place the soaked Ker Sangri in the pressure cooker, add 1 cup of water and cook the ker sangri until you hear 3 to 4 whistles.
1. After 3 to 4 whistles, turn off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. The next step is to combine it with spices and make the Ker Sangri SabziHeat a tablespoon of oil in a heavy bottomed pan over medium heat.
1. Add the ajwain, the red chillies and allow it to roast a little.
1. Add the cooked raisins, the turmeric powder, asafoetida powder, coriander, amchur powder and finally the Ker Sangri and give all the ingredients a good stir.
1. Stir fry until all the ingredients are combined and allow it to cook for another 5 minutes.
1. Once done, turn off the heat and the Ker Sangri Sabzi is ready to be served.
1. Serve the Ker Sangri Sabzi along with Kadhi, Phulkas and Steamed Rice and not to forget the ghee along with.
