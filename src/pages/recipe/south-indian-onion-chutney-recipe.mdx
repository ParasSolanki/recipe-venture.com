### Ingredients

- 2 Onions.
- 2 tablespoons Black Urad Dal (Split).
- 3 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 tablespoon Tamarind Paste.
- 2 teaspoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Jaggery.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the South Indian Onion Chutney recipe, firstly slice onions into medium pieces and keep aside.
1. Heat a teaspoon of oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, dry red chilies and sauté for a few seconds.
1. Add urad dal, saute and let it turn golden yellow.
1. Transfer this into a small dish and let it cool.
1. In the same pan, add a teaspoon of oil, add the chopped onion pieces and saute well for 4 to 5 minutes, until the raw odour of onion disappears.
1. Turn off the heat and cool.
1. Grind the urad dal, cumin seeds and red chillis to a coarse powder in a grinder.
1. Add to this, the fried onions, salt, tamarind and jaggery.
1. Grind to make a paste.
1. Add a little water (2-3 tablespoons) for a thinner consistency.
1. Do not add more water as it may turn out to be runny.
1. For seasoning, heat oil in a pan, add the mustard seeds and let them splutter.
1. Add the curry leaves and turn off the heat.
1. Pour this seasoning over the chutney.
1. Mix and savour this chutney with idlis or dosa’s.
