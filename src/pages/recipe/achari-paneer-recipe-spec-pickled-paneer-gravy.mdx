### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/4 tablespoon Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves).
- Water - as required.
- 2 tablespoons Pachranga Pickle - with masala.
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 inch Ginger.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 teaspoon Honey.
- 1/4 cup Fresh cream.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 2 Onion - chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1-1/2 cups Homemade tomato puree.
- 3 cloves Garlic.
- 200 grams Paneer (Homemade Cottage Cheese).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Achari Paneer recipe, grind all the ingredients to be ground into a paste - onion, ginger, garlic, pachranga pickle and fennel seeds.
1. Heat oil in a pan; add the ground paste and saute until the raw smell goes away and the oil starts separating.
1. This will take about 3 to 4 minutes.
1. Add the red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder and garam masala and saute into the masala.
1. Stir in the tomato puree, salt, honey and cream.
1. Give it a brisk boil.
1. Finally stir in the paneer into the achari masala.
1. You can add a little water if needed according to your preference and simmer the achari paneer masala for about 4 to 5 minutes so the flavors get combined well into the masala.
1. Once done, garnish Achari Paneer with Kasuri Methi leaves, check the taste and adjust the salt and spices accordingly and serve.
1. Serve Achari Paneer with Whole Wheat Lachha Paratha or Jeera Rice ,Lauki Raita and Apple Mawa Kheer for a weekend lunch.
