### Ingredients

- Sunflower Oil - for deep frying.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 cup Methi Leaves (Fenugreek Leaves).
- 1 cup Gram flour (besan).
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/4 cup Rice flour.
- 1/2 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds - crushed.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Methi Pakoras, sieve both the flours-besan & rice flour together with salt.
1. Add a ladle of hot oil (heated separately for 1 minute) and gently mix with a spoon.
1. Now add the methi leaves, salt, red chilli powder, hing, turmeric powder, ajwain and coriander seeds and mix well.
1. Methi leaves would leave out water in 2-3 mins.
1. Add water very slowly and make a thick batter.
1. Once it is done, adjust the spice and salt according to your taste.
1. Pour the rest of the oil in a pan on medium flame.
1. Once it is hot enough, use a greased spoon or wet hands to drop small pakoras (or bite sized pieces) into the hot oil.
1. Be sure not to overcrowd the frying pan (or kadai).
1. Fry on medium heat turning the batch over in the oil occasionally.
1. Fry till golden brown and drain the pakoras on the kitchen towel.
1. Fry the rest of the pakoras similarly.
1. Serve the Methi Pakoras with hot Masala Chai or Papaya Banana smoothie.
