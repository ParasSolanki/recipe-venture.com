### Ingredients

- Salt - as required.
- 1/2 cup Methi Leaves (Fenugreek Leaves) - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 1 Dry Red Chilli.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - for garnish if required.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- Sunflower Oil - for frying the methi pakoras.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing) - Asafoetida.
- 1 cup Gram flour (besan).
- 2 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 2 tablespoons Ghee.
- 3 tablespoons Gram flour (besan).
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/4 teaspoon Enos fruit salt - or baking powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Methi Pakoda Kadhi Recipe, first step is to make the pakora.
1. Take a large bowl and mix all the ingredients for the pakoras like - Gram Flour, Red Chili powder, methi leaves, onion, Turmeric powder, Enos Fruits salt, Asafoetida, Salt and Carom seeds.
1. Add little water at a time and make a thick pakora batter.
1. Heat a paniyaram pan, add a few drops of oil into the cavities and spoon small portions of the pakora batter into the cavities and pan fry them until it is brown and crisp on all sides.
1. Once the methi pakoras are done, keep them aside.
1. The next step is to make the Kadhi.
1. In a saucepan add the curd, gram flour, turmeric powder, red chilli powder and turmeric powder and salt.
1. Add 2-1/2 cups of water and mix the kadhi mixture well.
1. Turn the heat to high and keep whisking the kadhi until it becomes smooth and slightly thick.
1. Once the kadhi begins to thicken, turn the heat low and allow the kadhi to simmer for 10 minutes.
1. This simmering process allows the kadhi to get all the flavors.
1. After 10 minutes of simmering, add the pakoras into the kadhi and simmer the pakoras for another 5 to 8 minutes until they become soft.
1. While the Methi Pakoda Kadhi is simmering, heat ghee in a tadka pan; add the cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, and red chillies.
1. Allow them to crackle.
1. Give the Methi Kadhi Pakoda  a taste and adjust the salt and spices accordingly.
1. Once done, transfer the Methi Pakoda Kadhi to a serving bowl.
1. Pour the tadka over the Methi Pakoda Kadhi and give it a swirl and serve hot.
1. Serve the Methi Pakoda Kadhi Recipe along with Multigrain Methi Thepla, Aloo Simla Mirch Ki Sabzi and salad by the side for a delicious lunch or dinner.
