### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 300 grams Paneer (Homemade Cottage Cheese) - cut into cubes.
- 2 Coal - for coal smoking method.
- 1 cup Pickled onions.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf) - roasted ground to powder.
- 2 Saffron strands.
- 1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Sunflower Oil - for tossing.
- 1 inch Ginger.
- Salt - to taste.
- 6 Mint Leaves (Pudina).
- 3/4 teaspoon Black pepper powder.
- 3/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1 cup Hung Curd (Greek Yogurt).
- 3 cloves Garlic.
- 3 tablespoons Gram flour (besan).
- 1/4 teaspoon Black Salt (Kala Namak).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Smoked Tandoori Paneer Tikka Recipe, In a bowl, combine yogurt and gram flour making a smooth paste.
1. Using a pestle and mortar, pound the ginger, garlic, and mint leaves to make a coarse paste.
1. Add in the fennel seeds and ajwain and pound until blended.
1. Add this to the yogurt mixture.
1. Add in the remaining marinade ingredients into the yogurt mixture and stir to combine well.
1. Place the paneer cubes in a large bowl, add in the marinade and allow it to rest covered for about half an hour to one hour or even overnight.
1. While the paneer marination is close to over, we will now prepare for the coal smoking method.
1. Take a piece or two of coal (about 3 inches big) and hold it over a gas flame with the help of tongs.
1. The coal will begin to turn red hot and also grey in about 5 minutes.
1. This indicates that the coal can be used for smoking.
1. Place this hot coal inside a crack proof bowl.
1. At this point heat a large wok or a skillet with rim that can be covered on medium heat.
1. Add a little oil and place a few pieces of marinated paneer along with a little of the marinade.
1. If you have a stove top grill or a skillet, then arrange the paneer pieces and pour a little of the marinade over each of them.
1. Place the coal along with the bowl over the paneer.
1. Add a spoon of ghee or oil on the coal.
1. It will begin to smoke.
1. Immediately cover the pan and reduce the heat to low and let it sit for about a minute until the smoke has gone away.
1. Open the lid and remove the coal cup.
1. You dish will get a smoked flavored paneer that taste just like the Tandoori Paneer Tikkas.
1. Serve the Smoked Tandoori Paneer Tikka Recipe as a Party Appetizer with your meal of Dal Makhani, Paneer Butter Masala, Jeera Rice and Phulka.
