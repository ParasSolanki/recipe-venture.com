### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder.
- 1 teaspoon Tamarind Paste.
- 2 teaspoon Garlic - chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 3 Pearl onions (Sambar Onions).
- 2 Green Chillies.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Lemon juice.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 3/4 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 250 grams Prawns.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1/2 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 3 teaspoon Mustard oil.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 cup Coconut milk.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Malabar Prawn Curry recipe, wash and clean the prawns.
1. Marinate the prawn with 1 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon red chilli powder, 1 teaspoon salt and a juice of half a lemon for 30 minutes.
1. Take 1 cup of grated coconut and pour over 1 cup of boiling water and extract the coconut milk through a sieve.
1. Grind all the ingredients marked under ‘For the Paste’ with some water and keep it aside in a grinder.
1. In a heavy bottomed pan, fry the prawns on both sides in mustard oil for 3 to 4 minutes.
1. Remove and keep aside.
1. In the same pan, add a little more oil, when it is hot, add the mustard and fenugreek seeds, once they start to splutter, add the curry leaves, dry red chillies, and the onions.
1. Fry till the onions turns a little brown and then add the ground masala.
1. Saute for 5 to 6 minutes or till oil comes out from it on medium heat.
1. Add salt and tamarind paste and cook for further 3 to 4 minutes.
1. Now add the prawns and cook for 3 to 4 minutes and then add the coconut milk.
1. Let it comes to a boil and then lower the heat and let the curry cook for 2 to 3 minutes more.
1. Switch off the gas and keep it covered to trap the aroma of the curry inside.
1. Serve Malabar Prawn Curry with hot steamed rice and any raita of your choice for a delicious meal.
