### Ingredients

- Salt - to taste.
- 1/4 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/4 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 3 sprig Curry leaves - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Ginger - chopped.
- 1/4 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 tablespoons Green Chillies - chopped.
- 1 cup Tamarind Water - from 40 grams of tamarind.
- 3 tablespoons Jaggery.
- 1 tablespoon Sesame (Gingelly) Oil.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Puli Inji recipe, heat a tablespoon of oil in a saucepan; add the mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds and allow it to crackle.
1. Stir in the asafoetida, curry leaves, ginger and green chillies.
1. Saute for a few seconds.
1. Add the turmeric powder, tamarind extract, red chilli powder, jaggery and salt to taste.
1. With heat on high, allow the puli inii mixture to come to boil.
1. Once it comes to a boil, turn the heat to low and simmer the the puli inji for about 15 minutes until the mixture becomes a little thick.
1. At this point, turn off the heat and check the salt and adjust to taste accordingly.
1. Transfer the Puli inji to a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. The Puli Inji can be stored in an air tight container in the refrigerator for a couple of weeks and used as and when desired.
1. Serve Puli Inji with almost everything like dosa, idli, chilla, and especially with Upma Kozhukattai.
