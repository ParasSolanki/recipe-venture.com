### Ingredients

- 250 grams Bhindi (Lady Finger/Okra) - sliced in small round shape.
- Salt - as required.
- 1 Green Chilli - finely chopped.
- Water - as required.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/2 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 2 Onions - finely chopped.
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 tablespoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1-1/2 tablespoon Gram flour (besan).
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Sunflower Oil - as required.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Kurkuri Bhindi And Onion Masala recipe, Firstly we will make the kurkuri bhindi.
1. Take a mixing bowl, add the sliced bhindi, besan, 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon amchur powder, required salt and a little water.
1. Mix everything properly.
1. Once it is done, heat oil in a wok and fry the bhindis.
1. Once done, take it out on the absorbent paper to absorb the extra oil.
1. Keep it aside.
1. Now heat oil in a different wok.
1. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, hing and green chillies.
1. After 10 to 15 seconds add in the ginger garlic paste.
1. Saute it for a minute and then add the chopped onions.
1. Cook till it becomes soft and translucent.
1. Add in the remaining red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder and the required salt.
1. Mix it, cover the kadai and let it cook for 3 to 4 minutes.
1. Add in the fried bhindi and mix it properly.
1. Cook for two more minutes, switch off the stove and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
1. Serve Kurkuri Bhindi And Onion Masala along with Panchmel dal and Phulka for a weekday meal.
