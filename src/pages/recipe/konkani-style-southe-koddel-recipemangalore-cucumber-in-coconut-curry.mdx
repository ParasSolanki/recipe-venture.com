### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Sunflower Oil - for cooking.
- Salt - to taste.
- 3 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 cloves Garlic.
- 1 cup Tamarind Water.
- 1 Mangalorean cucumber - skin can be kept also.
- 4 tablespoon Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 tablespoon Jaggery.
- 1 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1 tablespoon White Urad Dal (Split).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Konkani Style Southe Koddel Recipe by first pressure cooking the mangalore cucumber with a pinch of salt and 1/2 cup water in a pressure cooker for 1 whistle.
1. Release the pressure naturally and then keep it aside.
1. Now heat a frying pan, add oil and the, fenugreek seeds, urad dal, chana dal, coconut, cumin seeds, dry red chillies, curry leaves, garlic.
1. Fry them for about 2 minutes till the aroma is released.
1. Once done, cool it down and then grind it into a smooth creamy paste by adding water little, After grinding keep it aside.
1. Now, heat a skillet and add oil, add the ground mixture along with the cooked Mangalore cucumber.
1. Stir well.
1. Add salt and turmeric powder and check for seasoning.
1. Leave it to simmer for 10 minutes.
1. In another kadai pan, heat oil and then crackle the mustard seeds and urad dal and immediately add the curry leaves and allow it to crackle and pour it over the curry and serve.
1. Serve the Konkani Style Southe Koddel Recipe along with Mangalorean Neer Dosa Recipe  and Solkadhi Recipe (Konkani Style Kokum Kadhi) as a refreshing drink at the end.
