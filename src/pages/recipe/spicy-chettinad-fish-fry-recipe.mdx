### Ingredients

- 4 Seer fish - steaks.
- 30 grams Tamarind - soaked in 1-2 tablespoon water.
- 1 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- Salt - to taste.
- 10 Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 teaspoon Sunflower Oil - adjustable.
- 8 cloves Garlic - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 1 teaspoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 2 Lemon wedges.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 Tomato - chopped.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- Sunflower Oil - to fry.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Chettinad Fish Fry Recipe, wash the seer fish steaks and keep it aside.
1. Heat a Tadka pan and dry roast curry leaves, garlic and the spices till a nice aroma is released.
1. After the spices cool down, using a Mortar and Pestle to grind these spices by adding a little oil.
1. Now add tomato and tamarind along with its soaked water, the ground spices, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chilli powder and salt to a Mixer.
1. Grind it into a thick paste.
1. The paste will be dark brown in colour.
1. Check salt, adjust and mix well.
1. Smear every seer fish steak with this spicy paste on both sides.
1. Be generous while smearing the paste.
1. Let it be thick to achieve the spicy Chettinad effect.
1. Marinate these steaks for at least 20 minutes.
1. After 20 minutes, heat a Non-stick pan for shallow frying.
1. Add adequate oil for shallow frying and place the fish steaks on the pan.
1. After 5 minutes, carefully flip the fish to the other side on the pan.
1. Continue to fry till the fish gets cooked and crisp.
1. The fish will appear flaky when itâ€™s cooked.
1. Garnish Spicy Chettinad Fish Fry Recipe with lime juice, garnish with lemon wedges and serve hot.
1. Serve Chettinad Fish Fry Recipe along with Steamed Rice and Chettinad Paruppu Urundai Kuzhambu to make it a complete meal.
