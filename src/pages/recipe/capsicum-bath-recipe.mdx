### Ingredients

- Salt - as required.
- 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split).
- Lemon juice - few drops.
- Fistful Green peas (Matar).
- 2-3 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- Few Cashew nuts.
- Few Curry leaves.
- 1 Onion - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 Green Chilli - slit.
- 2-3 Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - chopped into medium size pieces.
- 1 Dry Red Chilli - broken.
- 2-3 tablespoons Dry coconut (kopra) - grated.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2-3 tablespoons Vangi Bath Powder.
- Tamarind - soaked in water.
- 1 cup Rice - cooked.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Capsicum Bath recipe, firstly wash rice and cook it with water and salt in a saucepan.
1. The cooked rice grains should be separate and not mushy.
1. Once cooked, spread the cooked rice on a wide plate.
1. This will make the grains separate further and also helps to cool down quickly.
1. (It has to be cooled properly before using)Heat oil in a kadhai and add mustard seeds, lentils and cashew nuts.
1. Once they splutter add curry leaves, red chili and green chili .
1. Add onion, green peas, turmeric and salt.
1. Saute it till the raw smell of onion goes away.
1. Once it is done, add chopped capsicum and cook it for 3-4 minutes.
1. Once the capsicum is cooked half, add vangibath powder, tamarind juice and mix everything well.
1. Cover and cook for 3-4 minutes, so that capsicum takes in the flavour.
1. Finally, add the grated coconut and give a gentle mix.
1. Switch off after the extra moisture has evaporated.
1. Now add rice to the pan and mix everything properly.
1. Drizzle some lemon juice, salt and mix it well again.
1. Serve Capsicum Bath with Lauki Raita, Beetroot Raita, Burani Raita or any other Raita of your choice.
