### Ingredients

- 1 cup Elephant yam (Suran/Senai/Ratalu) - cut into small pieces.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 2 Dry Red Chilli.
- Sunflower Oil - for cooking.
- Salt - to taste.
- 18 grams Tamarind.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 4 tablespoon Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 cup Black Eyed Beans (Lobia) - Soaked for 2 hours in water.
- 1 tablespoon Jaggery.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 1 pinch Asafoetida (hing).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Konkani Style Alasande Randayi Recipe, soak the beans and keep it ready.
1. Heat a small skillet, dry roast the coriander seeds and set aside in a mixer.
1. Add the grated coconut, dry red chilies, and tamarind and grind it into a smooth mixture.
1. Heat a pressure cooker with oil, add the mustard seeds and allow it to crackle for 2 seconds and then add the curry leaves and hing.
1. Immediately add the paste that was ground, along with a teaspoon of turmeric powder and jaggery.
1. Give it a saute for 2 minutes.
1. Later add the chopped yam along with black eyed peas, add salt and check for seasoning.
1. Close the lid and pressure cook for about 3 whistle until it is done.
1. Switch off the heat and allow it to rest till all the pressure is released by itself.
1. Serve the Konkani Style Alasande Randayi Recipe along with Sukka and steamed rice or Homemade Crispy Ghee Roast Dosas.
