### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Sugar - optional.
- Water - as required.
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 cup Barnyard Millet.
- 2 Potatoes (Aloo) - peeled and chopped.
- 3 Kokum (Malabar Tamarind) - pieces.
- 3 tablespoon Roasted Peanuts (Moongphali) - powdered.
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped for garnish.
- 3 Green Chillies - chopped (or to taste).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Bhagar Recipe, first dry roast the bhagar (barnyard millet in a heavy bottom Wok/Kadai on a low heat, until you get a nice aroma.
1. Remove onto a plate and keep aside.
1. Heat ghee in the same wok.
1. Add cumin seeds and once they sizzle, add the green chillies and saut√© for a few seconds.
1. After a few seconds, add chopped potatoes, peanut powder, kokum and mix everything properly.
1. After 2 to 3 minutes, add water, salt, sugar (optional) and bring it to a boil.
1. Once the water boils, add the roasted bhagar, stirring continuously.
1. Cook covered on medium heat till it is well done and soft.
1. (About 10-12 minutes).
1. Switch off gas and keep covered for few minutes more.
1. Mix once again.
1. Garnish Bhagar Recipe (Maharashtrian style spicy Barnyard Millet) with coriander leaves and serve hot.
1. You can serve Bhagar along with plain Yogurt, Tomato Onion Cucumber Raita or any other raita of your choice and a roasted papad.
