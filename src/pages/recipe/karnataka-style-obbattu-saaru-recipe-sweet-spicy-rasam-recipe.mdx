### Ingredients

- 1 Onion - sliced.
- 4 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1/4 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 teaspoon Poppy seeds.
- 2 teaspoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 2 sprig Curry leaves.
- 2 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- 1/4 cup Jaggery.
- 2 tablespoon Dry coconut (kopra).
- 1 cup Tamarind Water.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1/4 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 2 Tomatoes - chopped.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 1 pinch Asafoetida (hing).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Karnataka Style Obbattu Saaru Recipe, first cook the chana dal with 1-1/2 cups of water until soft and is well cooked.
1. Cook the dal in the pressure cooker for about 4 to 5 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Once done, open the cooker, drain any excess water from the dal and keep it aside.
1. Mash the dal well to make a coarse mixture.
1. Heat ghee in a pan on medium heat, add the jaggery and sugar and stir until the jaggery and sugar dissolve completely.
1. Stir in the cooked mixture, coconut and cardamom to the jaggery mixture until well combined.
1. Continue stirring the the Obbattu poornam until the mixture begins to thicken and leaves the sides of the pan.
1.  Keep aside.
1. For the Saaru /Rasam MixHeat a skillet and dry roast all the ingredients that is mentioned in the rasam powder list and grind them into a smooth powder.
1. Keep it in the blender itself.
1. In the same skillet, add oil and fry the curry leaves, onions and tomatoes.
1. Fry them till the raw smell goes away.
1. Once done cool it down and grind them along with the rasam spice powder to a smooth paste.
1. To make the Obbattu Saaru recipeHeat a kadai, add 2 cups water, tamarind water, the poornam dal mix and bring it to boil.
1. Then add the rasam spice mixture, boil the Obbattu Saaru in on medium heat for about 20 minutes.
1. To temperHeat a small tempering pan with oil, splutter mustard seeds and hing.
1. Add this tempering over the Obbattu Saaru and serve with some chopped coriander leaves.
1. Check the salt and spices and adjust according to taste.
1. Serve the Karnataka Style Obbattu Saaru Recipe along with Steamed rice and Karnataka Style Sorakaya Palya Recipe.
