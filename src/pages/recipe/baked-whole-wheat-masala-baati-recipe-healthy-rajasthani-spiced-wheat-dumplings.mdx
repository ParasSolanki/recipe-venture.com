### Ingredients

- 2 tablespoon Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 2 Green Chillies.
- 1/2 cup Ghee.
- 1 cup Green peas (Matar).
- 1 teaspoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 3/4 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 cup Whole Wheat Flour.
- 1 Onion.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1/2 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Whole Wheat Masala Baati, first mix all ingredients mentioned under the ‘For baatis’ in a large mixing bowl.
1. Knead them all together to form a soft dough that is pliable, but not sticky.
1. If the dough turns out a touch hard to stiff, add a little milk and knead it till soft to touch.
1. Divide the dough into equal sized portions, place them in a platter or tray.
1. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for half an hour.
1. Meanwhile proceed to prepare the masala filling.
1. For this, place a pan on the heat.
1. Add some oil to it and allow it to get warm.
1. Then add cumin seeds, onions and green chilies to the oil and sauté until onions turn light pink.
1. Next, add ginger garlic paste, red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, roasted cumin powder and salt to the onions and toss it together.
1. Stir continuously as it cooks, until the raw smell of the spices goes away.
1. Then add peas and cook for 3 minutes until all the ingredients have combined together.
1. Add water to cook the peas, if required, but once they are cooked, cook it till the excess water has evaporated completely.
1. Once peas is cooked and water is completely dry, turn off the heat and set the masala aside so it can cool completely.
1. Now, to make the baatis.
1. Take one portion at a time, and flatten it into a small disc about 2-3 inches in diameter, and keep it in your palm.
1. Scoop some peas masala filling, place it in the center of the disc.
1. Then, bring the ends together, encasing the filling in the centre, seal the opening by pinching the edges together.
1. Press the ball slightly and make a small depression with your thumb, in the center of the ball.
1. Repeat with all the portions of dough.
1. Then, place a heavy bottomed pot on the heat with about 3 litres of water in it.
1. Bring the water to a boil, turn the heat down and gently slide the baatis into the water.
1. Allow them to cook on a low heat for 15 to 20 minutes until they cook and rise to the top.
1. Meanwhile, pre-heat the oven at 200 degrees centigrade.
1. When the baatis are cooked, drain them gently and place them in a platter to cool.
1. Once cooled, place the battis on on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and place the sheet in the oven.
1. Bake the masala stuffed battis at 200 degrees for about 25 to 35 minutes, turning them over every 10 minutes to ensure an evenly baked through and golden brown in color.
1. Once they’re golden brown and crisp, remove the masala battis baking sheet from the oven and place it on a baking tray to cool slightly.
1. Serve the Baked Whole Wheat Masala Baati while still hot, with ghee.
1. If you are calorie conscious, you can have it with Panchmel Dal, with just a light drizzle of ghee as a meal, or as a light evening snack along with a cup of tea.
