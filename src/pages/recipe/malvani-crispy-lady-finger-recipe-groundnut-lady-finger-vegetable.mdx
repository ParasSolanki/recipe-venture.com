### Ingredients

- 1 Stone flower.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 2 Dry Red Chilli.
- 1/2 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 500 grams Bhindi (Lady Finger/Okra) - washed and cut into small pieces.
- 1/4 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1 Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1/4 cup Roasted Peanuts (Moongphali) - roasted and crushed.
- 1/4 teaspoon Nagkesar/cassia buds.
- 1/2 teaspoon Ajwain (Carom seeds).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 3 Cloves (Laung).
- 2 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 Star anise.
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1/4 teaspoon Nutmeg powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Malvani Kurkuri Bhindi Recipe, we first prepare the malvani masala.
1. Heat a small pan and dry roast the ingredients mentioned in the malvani masala table in low heat.
1. Once  cool, grind it to a fine powder using a hand blender and keep aside.
1. In the same pan dry roast the de skinned peanuts in low heat and once its cool, roughly pound the peanuts using a mortar and pestle and keep aside.
1. Now, heat oil in a wide pan on medium flame.
1. Add the cut bhindi to the oil and saute until it is half cooked and becomes non sticky.
1. This will take around 10 minutes.
1. Now, add the freshly prepared malvani masala, season with salt and saute until the bhindi is fully cooked and turns crispy.
1. Switch off the flame, add the crushed roasted peanuts and mix well.
1. Serve the Malvani Kurkuri Bhindi Recipe along with Steamed Rice and Dal Tadka for a simple yet yummy weeknight dinner.
