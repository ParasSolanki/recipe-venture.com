### Ingredients

- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1/2 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal) - soaked for 20 minutes.
- Salt - to taste.
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped for garnish.
- 5 Cashew nuts.
- 2 tablespoons Roasted Gram Dal (Pottukadalai).
- 1 teaspoon Poppy seeds.
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 2 teaspoons Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 7 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- Sunflower Oil - for frying the pakodas.
- 1 Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 Green Chillies - finely chopped.
- 1 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.
- 2 tablespoons Fresh coconut.

### Instructions

1. To begin making Chettinad Pakoda Curry Recipe, we first prepare making the batter for the pakodas.
1. Soak the channa dal along with red chillies for 20 minutes and then grind it with fennel seeds, salt using a mixer grinder to a coarse paste with very little water.
1. Now, heat oil in a pan, and drop a spoon full of the pakoda mix into the oil and fry until golden brown in medium heat.
1. Remove it on a kitchen towel and keep aside.
1. Like wise, prepare the next batch of the pakodas with the remaining batter and keep aside.
1. Now, grind the ingredients mentioned in the 'to grind' table to a smooth paste using a mixer grinder and keep aside.
1. To prepare the gravy, heat oil in a pan, add the bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cardamom and let it release its aroma into the oil.
1. Add the fennel seeds, curry leaves and let it splutter.
1. Add the garlic and fry until golden brown.
1. Add the finely chopped onions and saute until transparent.
1. Add the tomatoes, green chillies, a pinch of saute and saute until the tomatoes are mushy.
1. Add the coriander and red chilli powder and saute for a minute.
1. Now, add the ground paste, 1.
1. 5 cups of water, salt and simmer until the masala paste is cooked well and the gravy comes together.
1. Add in the prepared pakodas and simmer for another 5 minutes.
1. The gravy will eventually be watery, but the pakodas will soak up the water and thicken the gravy.
1. Switch off the flame and garnish with coriander leaves.
1. Serve the Chettinad Pakoda Curry Recipe along with Tawa Parathas or Roti's, Steamed Rice, Poondu Rasam and Papad.
