### Ingredients

- 1 Dry Red Chilli.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Curry leaves - a few.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 2 teaspoons Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/2 teaspoon Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 2 Green Chillies - slit.
- 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 2 Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum) - chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Sunflower Oil.
- 3 tablespoons Gram flour (besan).
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 pinch Asafoetida (hing).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Bhopli Mirchi Chi Peeth Perun Bhaji Recipe, prep up all the ingredients required.
1. Dry roast besan till aromatic, on low medium heat.
1. Transfer to a bowl and keep aside.
1. In a pan/kadai, heat oil.
1. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds and let them crackle.
1. When they splutter, add hing, curry leaves and red chilli.
1. Later add green chillies and onion.
1. Fry till they turn translucent.
1. Next add capsicum.
1. Cook uncovered for about 5-6 minutes stirring at intervals.
1. When capsicum is crispy but a bit cooked, add turmeric powder, salt, red chilli powder, amchoor powder, coriander powder and cumin powder.
1. Mix well.
1. After a few minutes, sprinkle besan little by little, 1 tablespoon at a time such that it does not get lumpy.
1. Combine well so that no lumps are formed.
1. Then gradually sprinkle the rest of the gram flour while mixing the sabzi well.
1. After you have mixed in all 3 tablespoons of besan, add 1-2 tablespoons of oil and garam masala powder.
1. Mix well.
1. Cook on medium high heat for another 3-4 minutes so that all masala is coated well to capsicum and besan.
1. you can sprinkle a little water to bring everything together, instead of oil and it works the same though.
1. Bhopli Mirchi Chi Peeth Perun Bhaji is an easy recipe that can be done within 30 minutes and be served as a side dish with Phulka and Panchmel Dal for your everyday meals.
1. You can pack them away for lunch or roll it in the Tawa Paratha to make rolls.
