### Ingredients

- 1 Carrot (Gajjar) - chopped.
- 6 Green beans (French Beans) - chopped.
- 1/4 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Jaggery.
- 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 Tomato - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Whole Black Peppercorns.
- 1/4 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal).
- 1 Potato (Aloo) - chopped.
- Salt - to taste.
- Water - as required for cooking.
- 4 Small Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - diced.
- 1 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 4 cloves Garlic - crushed.
- 4 cloves Garlic.
- 10 Pearl onions (Sambar Onions) - cut into wedges.
- 1 cup Tamarind Water.
- 1 pinch Asafoetida (hing).
- 2 teaspoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- 2 teaspoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 6 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 cup Water.
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Udupi Style Mixed Vegetable Sambar, we will firstly chop all the vegetables and keep them ready.
1. Next, boil the toor dal along with all the other vegetables (eggplant, potato, carrot, green beans), salt, turmeric powder and water in a pressure cooker for 4 whistles.
1. Release the pressure naturally.
1. Once done mash it lightly.
1. The next step is to make the sambar powder.
1. Heat a heavy bottomed pan and add coriander seeds, cumin seeds, dry red chillies, whole black pepper corns and fenugreek seeds, Dry roast them for a minute and switch off the heat.
1. Once it is cooled down, add this mixture into a mixer jar along with garlic and coconut.
1. Grind them into a fine powder.
1. Heat a sauce pan with oil, add pearl onions.
1. Saute the pearl onions till they are soft and translucent.
1. Once the onions are soft, add the tomatoes along with toor dal and vegetable mixture.
1. Add about 1 cup of water and adjust the consistency according to your preference.
1. Once it starts to boil, add jaggery, 2 tablespoons of sambar powder, salt to taste and check for seasoning.
1. Bring it to a boil.
1. Let it cook for about 2 to 3 minutes.
1. Switch off the heat.
1. The next step is to temper the sambar.
1. For tempering, heat oil in a tadka pan.
1. Add mustard seeds, cloves, asafoetida, dry red chilies, curry leaves and let it cook for about 20 seconds.
1. Pour the seasoning over the sambar and mix it well.
1. It is ready to be served.
1. Serve the Udupi Style Mixed Vegetable Sambar Recipe along with some Steamed Rice, Cabbage Thoranand Elai Vadam by the side to enjoy your comforting lunch meal.
