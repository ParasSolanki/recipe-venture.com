### Ingredients

- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 tablespoons Dhansak masala.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 Tomato - finely chopped.
- 6 Small Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - sliced into roundels.
- 4 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 1 Ridge Gourd (Turai/ Peerkangai) - peeled sliced into roundels.
- 1 tablespoon Jaggery.
- 1-1/2 teaspoons Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).
- 1 Onion - thinly sliced.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Turai Baingan Ki Sabzi Recipe, keep the ingredients ready.
1. Soak the cut brinjal in water with little salt to prevent discoloration.
1. Heat oil in a wide pan; add the sliced brinjal, sprinkle some salt and roast the brinjal until cooked.
1. You can cover the pan and cook the brinjal until done.
1. Keep this aside.
1. Make the fresh Dhansak Masala using the recipe on the link.
1.  Keep aside.
1. Heat a skillet with oil over medium heat, add garlic and onions and saute until they turn a little soft.
1.  Add in chopped tomatoes and give it a mix.
1. Once the tomatoes turn lightly mushy, add the Dhansak Masala and the cut turai.
1. Sprinkle salt, amchur powder, turmeric powder and cover the pan and cook the turai until done.
1. The turai will release a lot of water, while it is cooking, hence there is no need to add additional water for it to cook.
1. Once the turai is cooked, add the roasted baingan into the sabzi and stir well.
1. Stir in the jaggery into the Turai Baingan Ki Sabzi and check the spice and salt and adjust according to taste.
1. Once done, turn off the heat, stir in the coriander leaves and transfer the Turai Baingan Ki Sabzi to a serving bowl and serve hot.
1. Serve the Turai Baingan Ki Sabzi along with Moong dal, Millet Phulka and Cauliflower & Cucumber Raita by the side.
