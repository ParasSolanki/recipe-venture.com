### Ingredients

- 2 Tomatoes - pureed.
- 1 Green Chilli - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 2 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 1/2 cup Yellow Moong Dal (Split) - washed and soaked.
- 2 Onions - finely chopped.
- 2 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 cup Water.
- 2 Cloves (Laung).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2 teaspoon Ginger - chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Mint Leaves (Pudina) - chopped.
- 2 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Maakhmi Dal, wash and soak the moong dal for 30 minutes.
1. In a pressure cooker, heat ghee on medium flame, add the cinnamon, cloves and cardamom.
1. To this add onions and fry until they turn into a nice deep golden brown colour.
1. Once the onions get the nice brown colour, add the turmeric powder and red chili powder.
1. Mix well.
1. Add the tomato puree at this stage and cook well, until the onion-tomato mix comes together.
1. To this onion-tomato bhuna masala add coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, chopped pudina leaves and add salt as required.
1. Add the soaked and drained dal, and water and close the pressure cooker.
1. Pressure cook for 2 whistles and turn off the flame.
1.  Allow the pressure to release naturally.
1. Once you open the pressure cooker, mash the dal with the potato masher and mix well.
