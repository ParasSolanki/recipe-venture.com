### Ingredients

- 10 grams Tamarind.
- 3 Carrots (Gajjar) - peeled and cut into big chunks.
- 1/4 cup Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 2 teaspoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1/4 cup Arhar dal (Split Toor Dal) - toor dal.
- 2 teaspoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Ghee - for cooking.
- 4 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 4 cloves Garlic - finely chopped.
- 2 Tomatoes - chopped.
- 250 grams Green beans (French Beans) - chopped to 1/2 an inch pieces.
- 1 teaspoon Rice.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 1-1/2 teaspoons White Urad Dal (Split).
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 Brinjal (Baingan / Eggplant) - cut into big chunks.
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Kodava Kootu Curry Recipe - Mixed Vegetable Stew, chop all the vegetables and keep it ready.
1. Heat a flat skillet on medium heat, drizzle some ghee add in mustard seeds an cumin seeds and allow it to crackle for few seconds.
1. Add coriander seeds, chana dal, urad dal, uncooked rice, dry red chillies and saute for few minutes till they are lightly roasted.
1. Add in grated coconut and turmeric powder and saute till the coconut have lightly roasted.
1. Once done, cool them down and add all the ingredients into a mixer along with tamarind and little bit of water and grind it into a smooth paste.
1. Pressure cook the vegetables along with a little salt and 1/4 cup of water for 2 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. To make the Kootu Curry, keep a pressure cooker on over a medium heat.
1. Drizzle some oil add chopped tomatoes and saute till they turn slightly mushy.
1. Add in the ground coconut paste, keep stirring for couple of minutes, add the washed toor dal along with 1-1/2 cups of water, salt to taste and pressure cook the dal for 4 to 5 whistles and turn off the heat.
1. Allow the pressure to release naturally and stir in the cooked vegetables and give the kootu curry one last mix.
1. Check the salt and adjust to taste accordingly.
1. Once done, transfer the Kootu curry to a serving bowl.
1. To make the tempering, heat a tadka pan on medium heat, drizzle ghee, add in mustard seeds and allow it to splutter.
1. Add curry leaves and leave it to splutter and add garlic and pinch of hing, saute till they soften.
1. Once done pour this tempered ingredients over the Kodava Kootu curry and serve hot.
1. Serve the Kodava Kootu Curry Recipe - Mixed Vegetable Stew along with Mangalorean Neer Dosa  and Semiya Payasam for your weekend Breakfast.
