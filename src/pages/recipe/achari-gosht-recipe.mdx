### Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon Garam masala powder.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 Green Chilli.
- 2 teaspoons Sunflower Oil.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Kalonji (Onion Nigella Seeds).
- 1 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 2 Onions - sliced.
- Salt - adjust according to taste.
- 1 teaspoon Fennel seeds (Saunf).
- 2 Dry Red Chillies.
- 1/2 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt).
- 2 Tomatoes - chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Methi Seeds (Fenugreek Seeds).
- 300 grams Mutton.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Achari Gosht Recipe, in a large bowl add the curd, red chilli powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder and turmeric powder and mix them well together.
1. Place the mutton pieces in this curd mixture and coat the mutton with the curd/marinade mixture.
1. Keep it aside for at least 1 hour.
1. In a mixer, add onions, tomatoes and green chillies and grind to a smooth paste and keep it aside.
1. In a skillet, add all the ingredients to prepare the achari masala and roast the whole spices on a medium flame for 10 minutes until the seeds start popping out.
1. Take the skillet off the heat and let the spices cool to room temperature.
1. Grind this into a coarse powder in a mixer and keep it aside.
1. In a pressure cooker pan, heat some oil, add the ground tomato and onions mixture and saute till they lose their raw smell.
1. Add in the achari masala and saute for some more time.
1. Add in the marinated mutton pieces and keep cooking till the curd loses its moisture.
1. Check for salt and add about 1 cup of water and pressure cook for atleast 5 whistles.
1. Serve the Achari Gosht Recipe along with Garlic Naan Without Yeast Recipe and pickled onions and Tadka Dal to make it a complete dish.
