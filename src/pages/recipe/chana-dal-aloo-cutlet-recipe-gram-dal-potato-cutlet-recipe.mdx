### Ingredients

- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - a small bunch.
- Sunflower Oil - for making cutlets.
- 2 Potatoes (Aloo) - boiled and mashed.
- 1 tablespoon Gram flour (besan).
- 2 tablespoons Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 cup Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal) - soaked for 3 hours.
- Mint Leaves (Pudina) - a small bunch.
- 1 teaspoon Cardamom Powder (Elaichi).
- 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 inch Ginger - finely chopped.
- 2 Green Chilli - finely chopped.
- 1 teaspoon Chaat Masala Powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Chana Dal Aloo Cutlet recipe, first wash the chana dal and soak in water for 1 hour or more.
1. Heat a pressure cooker and add the chana dal and water, cook for 2 to 3 whistles.
1. Once done, strain the water from the chana dal and make a dry fine paste using a mixer grinder.
1. Heat a teaspoon of oil and temper with cumin seeds, asafoetida and saute it for about 10 seconds.
1. Add ginger, coconut, red chilli powder, cardamom powder.
1. Saute for a few seconds.
1. Add the chana dal paste, boiled mashed potatoes, besan, coriander leaves, mint leaves and chaat masala.
1. Now stir fry the dal mixture for about 2 to 3 minutes on medium to low flame until it thickens.
1. Once it thickens, turn off the heat.
1. Next, make flat thick medium balls and refrigerate the Chana Dal Aloo Cutlet for about 15 minutes.
1. Heat a frying pan with oil and fry the Chana Dal Aloo Cutlet from both side for 5 minutes each or till they turn golden brown in colour.
1. Make sure you fry on low to medium heat to get a perfect crispness.
1. Once done, take the Chana Dal Aloo Cutlet out on a kitchen towel to absorb the excess oil.
1. Serve hot.
1. Serve Chana Dal aloo Cutlet along with Dhaniya Pudina Chutney and a cup of Masala Chai or Filter Coffee for evening snacks.
