### Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 3 tablespoons Fresh coconut - grated.
- Sesame (Gingelly) Oil - for cooking.
- 1 tablespoon Coriander (Dhania) Seeds.
- Salt - to taste.
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds.
- 4 Long Green Brinjal - cut into 1 inch pieces.
- 1 tablespoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal).
- 3 Byadagi Dried Chillies.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 sprig Curry leaves.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Karnataka Style Badanekayi Palya Recipe, prepare all the ingredients and keep it ready.
1. To begin we will first roast and grind the ingredients in the "Masala to Grind".
1. Heat a small skillet, roast the chana dal on low heat until it is golden brown.
1. Once roasted, stir in the coriander seeds, cinnamon sticks and dry red chilli and roast until you get the aroma of coriander seeds.
1. Cool the mixture and grind to make a fine powder.
1. Heat a skillet with oil, add teaspoon of mustard seeds and allow it to crackle.
1. Add in the asafoetida, curry leaves and stir for a few seconds.
1. Add the chopped brinjal, sprinkle some salt, turmeric powder, cover the pan and cook the brinjal until it has softened.
1. Once the brinjal has softened stir in the grated coconut along with the ground masala mix.
1. Combine and saute the ingredients well and simmer until the masalas are well incorporated into the Badanekayi (brinjal).
1. Add water if required if the palya becomes too dry.
1. Close the lid and cook the Badanekayi Palya and make sure to keep sauteing the gravy at regular intervals.
1. Once done serve warm.
1. Serve the Karnataka Style Badanekayi Palya Recipe as a side dish along with some Mixed Vegetable Sambar and Steamed Rice to make it a complete meal.
