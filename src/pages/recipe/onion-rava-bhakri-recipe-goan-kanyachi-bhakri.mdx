### Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - finely chopped.
- 1 Green Chilli - finely chopped.
- 2 tablespoons Fresh coconut - grated.
- 1 teaspoon Jaggery - crushed.
- Ghee - or oil to smear while cooking.
- 1 Onion - finely chopped.
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt.
- 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 1 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava).

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Onion Bhakri Recipe (Goan Kanyachi Bhakri), we will first  soak the rava in 1-1/2 cup of water for about 30 minutes.
1. After about 30 minutes, drain the excess water using a fine mesh colander.
1. Add the soaked rava to a large mixing bowl and add all the ingredients and stir well to combine.
1. Adjust the salt to suit your taste.
1. The next step is the cook the Onion Bhakri.
1. Grease and preheat a skillet over medium heat.
1. Add one ladleful of batter onto the skillet and spread it just a bit to make a thick pancake.
1. Turn the heat to low, cover the pan and cook the Onion Bhakri for about 5 minutes.
1. The process of covering the pan, allows the Onion Bhakri to steam cook and come together like a bread/pancake.
1. Half way through the cooking process, drizzle some ghee around the sides and the top.
1. Once you notice the top is cooked a little, flip over the cook the Onion Bhakri on the other side.
1. Cook until the Onion Bhakri is cooked through completely and is golden brown and crisp on both sides.
1. Serve the Onion Bhakri or Kanyachi Bhakri along with a mixed vegetable korma for breakfast, lunch or even a quick weeknight dinner.
