### Ingredients

- 2 tablespoon Sesame powder - roasted.
- 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - as required for garnish (chopped).
- 2 teaspoon Maharashtrian Goda Masala.
- Salt - to taste.
- 6 Gilka (sponge/silk squash) - cut into about 3 inch pieces.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 1 teaspoon Jaggery - grated.
- 2 Onions - finely chopped.
- 3 tablespoon Groundnut powder.
- 7 cloves Garlic - crushed.
- 1 tablespoon Sunflower Oil.
- 1/2 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 2 teaspoons Red Chilli powder.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Stuffed Gilka, we will first make the stuffing.
1. In a bowl mix all ingredients mentioned under stuffing.
1. Mix everything properly and keep it aside.
1. Next, make a slit length wise in each gilka piece without cutting through.
1. Carefully stuff about a tablespoon of the filling inside the cut.
1. Heat oil in a flat non-stick pan.
1. Add the cumin seeds and the place the stuffed gilka in the pan, cut side up.
1. Cover and cook for 3-4 minutes on low flame.
1. Carefully turn over the pieces and cook on the other side till soft but the pieces are still intact.
1. Use 1-2 tablespoon water if required for cooking.
1. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve.
1. Serve Stuffed Gilka with Dal Tadka, Kachumber Salad and Tawa Paratha for a spicy, flavourful meal.
