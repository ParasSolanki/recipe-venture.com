### Ingredients

- 2 tablespoon Ginger Garlic Paste.
- 2 Tomatoes - ripe.
- 1 teaspoon Cumin powder (Jeera).
- 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1/2 cup Coriander (Dhania) Leaves - chopped.
- 2 Bay leaf (tej patta).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 Cardamom (Elaichi) Pods/Seeds.
- 2 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- Salt - Salt to taste.
- 3 Dry Red Chilli.
- 5 Potatoes (Aloo) - peeled & quartered.
- 4 Whole Eggs - boiled and peeled.
- 2 tablespoon Sunflower Oil - preferable Mustard oil (adjustable).
- 3 Green Chillies - slit.
- 4 Cloves (Laung).
- 2 Onion - chopped or grounded.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the Aloo Konir Dom recipe, take potato pieces and boiled eggs in a bowl, sprinkle around 1 teaspoon of turmeric and 1-2 pinch of salt and coat the potatoes and eggs.
1. Keep aside.
1. Heat oil in a wok/ pan till smoking hot.
1. Add damped potatoes and fry them till golden in colour.
1. Now add eggs in the oil and gently fry till golden.
1. Transfer the potatoes and eggs in a plate and set aside.
1. In the remaining oil add bay leaf and all other whole spices along with dry red chilies.
1. Wait till they crackle, and then add chopped onions and saute them till golden in colour.
1. Add ginger garlic paste and slit green chilies and fry them with onion until brown in colour.
1. Now add in the chopped tomatoes along with cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder and garam masala powder.
1. Stir well and fry till oil starts leaving sides.
1. Add little water if you see the masala sticking for bottom.
1. Once the masala is fried nicely and the oil has oozed out from the sides, add fried potatoes and mix well.
1. Fry for another 2 mins and add water as per required consistency.
1. Adjust salt as needed and drop in the eggs in the curry.
1. Let the curry come to a boil, then simmer the flames and cover the lid.
1. Let the curry cook till potatoes are tender and soft.
1. Remove from stove, and garnish with plenty of chopped coriander and serve hot with plain steamed rice.
1. Serve Aloo Konir Dom along with Whole Wheat Lachha Paratha, Boondi Raita and Kachumber Salad for a weekday lunch or dinner.
