### Ingredients

- 2 to 3 Green Chillies - slit.
- 1 Onion - chopped (optional).
- 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder (Dhania).
- 1 inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini).
- Salt - to taste.
- 3/4 teaspoon Garam masala powder.
- 2 cups Hung Curd (Greek Yogurt) - beaten till smooth.
- 1-1/2 tablespoons Rice flour.
- 1/2 tablespoon Turmeric powder (Haldi).
- 2 to 3 Cloves (Laung).
- Red Chilli powder - as required.
- 2 to 3 tablespoons Mustard oil.
- 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing).
- 1 Black cardamom (Badi Elaichi).
- 1 tablespoon Ghee.
- 1-1/2 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera).
- 2 cups Kabuli Chana (White Chickpeas) - soaked overnight and boiled.
- 3 to 4 Whole Black Peppercorns.

### Instructions

1. To begin making the recipe, grind together black cardamoms, black peppercorns and cloves in a mixer grinder.
1. Make sure it doesnâ€™t turn into a fine powder, it should be granulated.
1. Mix rice flour with water and keep it aside.
1. Heat 2 tablespoons of mustard oil to smoking point on medium heat.
1. Add a pinch of asafoetida, cumin seeds and cinnamon.
1. Next, add all the dry spice which we grinded earlier.
1. Add onion and saute it for a minute (this step is optional).
1. Add garam masala powder and coriander powder.
1. Add cooked chickpeas and mix it well with the masala.
1. Add salt and chilli powder to taste.
1. Add some slit green chillies and let it cook for 2 to 4 minutes more.
1. Now turn the heat on low and add beaten yogurt while stirring chickpeas.
1. Keep stirring the mixture until yogurt mixes well.
1. Increase the heat to medium and continue stirring till yogurts starts to boil.
1. Stirring is important to avoid the yogurt from breaking up or cuddle.
1. Add rice flour mixture and ghee and keep stirring.
1. Cook it for another 5-6 minutes until it is cooked and reaches the right consistency.
1. Do remember that the gravy thickens as it cools down.
1. Serve Himachali Chana Madra with steamed rice and Kachumber Salad for a perfect meal.
